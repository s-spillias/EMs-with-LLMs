Assessment & Chosen Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                        
Current model comparisons with residuals indicate that while the temperature‐mediated coral growth and COTS reproduction capture some dynamics of boom–bust behavior, the coral logistic growth is oversimplified because it ignores    
interspecific competition between slow‐ and fast‑growing corals for limited space. In reality, these coral groups compete for the same substrate; hence, incorporating an interspecific competition effect in the coral logistic terms  
is ecologically justified. In our improvement, we add two new parameters (log_competition_slow and log_competition_fast) such that slow coral growth is further limited by fast coral density (scaled by exp(log_competition_slow)) and 
vice‐versa. This addition helps capture feedbacks in community composition that can influence the timing, magnitude, and duration of COTS outbreaks while avoiding data leakage by only using previous time‐step values.
