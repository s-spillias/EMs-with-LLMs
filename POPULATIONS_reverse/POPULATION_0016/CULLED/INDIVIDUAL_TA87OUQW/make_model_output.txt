Summary assessment & chosen improvement:                                           
Based on our evaluation of the current model, the residuals indicate that the model
under‐captures coral responses under high sea‑surface temperature conditions. In   
the project context, accurately capturing the boom–bust cycles of COTS outbreaks is
critically linked to coral‐health dynamics. Although the model already includes a  
temperature multiplier for coral growth, we propose adding an additional non‑linear
environmental stress effect on coral growth. This “stress factor” reduces coral    
growth rates when temperatures exceed the optimum, thereby accounting for bleaching
and stress‑induced reductions. Ecologically this mechanism is justified as elevated
temperatures can cause coral bleaching and limit recovery, which in turn influences
the predator–prey (COTS–coral) dynamics.                                           

The following changes will be made:                                                

 1 Update intention.txt                                                            
   Document our assessment and the rationale for adding the new environmental      
   stress parameter.                                                               
 2 Update parameters.json                                                          
   Add a new parameter, log_coral_env_sensitivity, with clear ecological           
   justification.                                                                  
 3 Update model.cpp                                                                
    • Add the new parameter declaration.                                           
    • Within the time‐loop, compute a new stress_factor (using previous time step  
      values only) that applies an additional exponential penalty when sea‑surface 
      temperature exceeds the optimum for coral growth.                            
    • Multiply both the slow and fast coral logistic growth terms by the           
      stress_factor.
