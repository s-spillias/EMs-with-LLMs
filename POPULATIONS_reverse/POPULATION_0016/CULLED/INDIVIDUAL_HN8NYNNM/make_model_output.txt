Assessment and Ecological Rationale                                                                                                                                                                                                     
The model currently captures COTS outbreak dynamics well with density‐dependent regulation and an Allee effect, and it incorporates cumulative damage for slow coral. However, fast coral currently lacks any mechanism to account for  
persistent tissue damage from intense COTS predation. Ecologically, although fast-growing, these coral species may still incur lasting damage that hinders their recovery after severe outbreaks. To improve the model’s realism without
compromising parsimony, we will add a new parameter (log_damage_fast) to represent cumulative damage in fast coral. This additional mechanism introduces a negative feedback on fast coral recovery due to past predation, thereby      
better capturing the boom‐bust cycles observed on the Great Barrier Reef while ensuring that only previous time-step values are used in predictions.
