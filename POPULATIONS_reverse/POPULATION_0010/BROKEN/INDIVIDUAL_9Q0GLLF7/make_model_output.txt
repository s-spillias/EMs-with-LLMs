 \section{Ecological Mechanisms}                 
                                                 
 The model has been enhanced to include coral    
 stress responses to Crown-of-thorns Starfish    
 (COTS) predation pressure. This addition        
 represents an important indirect pathway in     
 coral-COTS dynamics that was previously missing 
 from the model.                                 
                                                 
 When corals experience predation pressure from  
 COTS, they activate various defense mechanisms  
 including:                                      
 \begin{itemize}                                 
     \item Production of chemical deterrents     
     \item Enhanced nematocyst deployment        
     \item Increased mucus production            
 \end{itemize}                                   
                                                 
 These defensive responses require significant   
 energy allocation, reducing resources available 
 for growth and reproduction. This creates a     
 compound effect where high COTS densities impac 
 corals through both direct predation and reduce 
 growth capacity.                                
                                                 
 The stress response is modeled as a hyperbolic  
 function that reduces coral growth rates based  
 COTS predation pressure. The strength of this   
 effect is controlled by the stress_effect       
 parameter, which determines how severely coral  
 growth is suppressed under predation pressure.  
                                                 
 This mechanism helps explain the rapid decline  
 coral coverage during COTS outbreaks, as the    
 combination of direct predation and             
 stress-induced growth reduction can lead to     
 faster coral loss than predation alone would    
 predict.
