The current model uses a simple Monod

reality, as phytoplankton density    
increases, there can be interference

 3 Local nutrient depletion zones    
   around cells

 This model enhancement introduces   
 density-dependent competition effec 
 in phytoplankton nutrient uptake. A 
 phytoplankton density increases,    
 cells begin to interfere with each  
 other's ability to acquire nutrient 
 through multiple mechanisms:        
                                     
 1. Physical interference between    
 cells can reduce the effective      
 surface area available for nutrient 
 uptake                              
 2. Dense populations create boundar 
 layers that inhibit nutrient        
 diffusion                           
 3. Local depletion zones form aroun 
 cells in dense populations          
                                     
 The competition effect is implement 
 using a density-dependent reduction 
 factor in the nutrient uptake term: 
 competition_factor = 1 / (1 + c_P * 
 P)                                  
                                     
 where c_P is the competition        
 coefficient that determines how     
 strongly phytoplankton density      
 reduces uptake efficiency.          
                                     
 This mechanism provides negative    
 feedback on phytoplankton growth at 
 high densities, which better        
 represents the physical and chemica 
 constraints on nutrient acquisition 
 in dense populations.
