 =======                                    
 This model enhancement incorporates        
 phytoplankton nutrient storage (luxury     
 uptake) dynamics, which is a well-document 
 ecological process where phytoplankton can 
 accumulate nutrients beyond their immediat 
 growth requirements when nutrients are     
 abundant. This stored pool can later suppo 
 growth when external nutrients become      
 scarce.                                    
                                            
 The implementation uses Droop's cell quota 
 model framework, where growth depends on   
 internal nutrient status (Q) rather than   
 external nutrient concentration. Key       
 equations:                                 
                                            
 1. Nutrient uptake rate = f(external N,    
 Q_max - Q)                                 
    - Uptake slows as internal stores       
 approach maximum capacity                  
    - Maintains mass balance                
                                            
 2. Growth rate = r_max * (Q - Q_min)/(Q -  
 Q_min + K_Q)                               
    - Growth depends on how much internal   
 nutrient content exceeds minimum           
 requirements                               
    - Allows continued growth for some time 
 after external nutrients are depleted      
                                            
 This better represents the temporal        
 decoupling between nutrient uptake and     
 growth that occurs in natural systems,     
 potentially explaining the observed lag    
 between nutrient depletion and biomass     
 peaks.
