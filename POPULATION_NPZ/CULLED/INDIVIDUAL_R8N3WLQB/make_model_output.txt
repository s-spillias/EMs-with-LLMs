 The model has been enhanced to include pre 
 handling time limitations in the zooplankt 
 grazing functional response. This          
 modification better represents the         
 mechanical and physiological constraints   
 that limit maximum ingestion rates at high 
 prey densities.                            
                                            
 The original Holling Type II response      
 (P/(K_P + P)) assumes instantaneous prey   
 handling. The enhanced formulation (P/(K_P 
 P + g_max * h_time * P)) explicitly accoun 
 for the time predators spend handling each 
 prey item, which reduces their ability to  
 capture new prey. This creates a more      
 realistic saturating response where grazin 
 efficiency decreases at high phytoplankton 
 concentrations.                            
                                            
 This mechanism is particularly important f 
 capturing:                                 
 1. Reduced grazing pressure at high        
 phytoplankton densities, allowing bloom    
 formation                                  
 2. More gradual transitions between        
 resource-limited and saturation-limited    
 grazing regimes                            
 3. Better representation of the            
 predator-prey time lags that drive         
 population cycles                          
                                            
 The handling time parameter (h_time) is    
 based on empirical measurements of         
 zooplankton feeding mechanics and provides 
 mechanistic constraint on maximum ingestio 
 rates.
