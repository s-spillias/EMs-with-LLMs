 =======                                                                        
 This model incorporates nutrient-dependent phytoplankton uptake efficiency to  
 better represent how phytoplankton adapt their nutrient acquisition strategies 
 to environmental conditions. The uptake efficiency (Î·) varies with ambient     
 nutrient concentration following a Monod-type relationship:                    
                                                                                
 Î· = Î·_max * N / (K_Î· + N)                                                      
                                                                                
 where Î·_max is the maximum uptake efficiency and K_Î· is the half-saturation    
 constant for efficiency.                                                       
                                                                                
 This mechanism captures several important ecological processes:                
 1. At low nutrient concentrations, uptake efficiency increases, representing   
 enhanced nutrient acquisition strategies                                       
 2. At high concentrations, efficiency saturates, reflecting physiological      
 limitations                                                                    
 3. The variable efficiency creates a more realistic coupling between nutrients 
 and phytoplankton growth                                                       
                                                                                
 This addition helps explain the observed dynamics by allowing phytoplankton to 
 maintain growth under lower nutrient conditions, potentially improving the     
 model's ability to capture bloom dynamics and subsequent ecosystem responses.
