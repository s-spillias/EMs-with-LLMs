 This model incorporates luxury uptake of nutrients by phytoplankton, a key ecologic 
 mechanism where cells can store excess nutrients beyond their immediate growth      
 requirements. This storage capacity is represented by Q_store, which modifies       
 nutrient uptake rates based on the cell's current storage status.                   
                                                                                     
 The storage-dependent uptake mechanism uses:                                        
 - Q_min: Minimum cellular nutrient quota needed for survival                        
 - Q_max: Maximum storage capacity for nutrients                                     
 - Q_store: Current nutrient storage level per unit biomass                          
 - f_store: A function that reduces uptake as storage approaches maximum             
                                                                                     
 This better represents how phytoplankton can rapidly acquire nutrients when availab 
 and continue growing even as external nutrients decline, leading to more realistic  
 bloom dynamics and nutrient drawdown patterns.
