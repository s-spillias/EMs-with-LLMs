 This enhancement introduces temperature dependence to     
 phytoplankton growth dynamics using the Arrhenius equatio 
 which is widely used to model temperature effects on      
 biological rates. The modification affects the maximum    
 nutrient uptake rate (vmax) through a temperature scaling

 The Arrhenius equation is implemented as:                 
 \[ \text{temp\_scale} =                                   
 \exp\left(\frac{E}{k}\left(\frac{1}{T_{\text{ref}}} -

 where:                                                    
 \begin{itemize}                                           
 \item $E$ is the activation energy (eV)                   
 \item $k$ is the Boltzmann constant (8.617333262145×10⁻⁵  
 eV/K)                                                     
 \item $T_{\text{ref}}$ is the reference temperature (293. 
 K = 20°C)                                                 
 \item $T$ is the ambient temperature in Kelvin            
 \end{itemize}                                             
                                                           
 This modification captures the fundamental biochemical    
 constraint that metabolic rates typically increase        
 exponentially with temperature within the biological      
 temperature range. The reference temperature is set to    
 20°C, a common baseline for phytoplankton growth studies.

 \[ \text{uptake} = v_{\text{max}} \cdot \text{temp\_scale 
 \cdot \frac{N \cdot P}{k_m + N} \]                        
                                                           
 This mechanistic relationship should better capture:      
 \begin{itemize}                                           
 \item Seasonal variations in phytoplankton growth         
 \item The timing and magnitude of bloom events            
 \item Temperature-dependent metabolic scaling
