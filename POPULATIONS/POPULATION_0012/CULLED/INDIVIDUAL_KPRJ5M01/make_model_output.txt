 Project Assessment:                                                                                                                   
 The current model fairly captures outbreak initiation and decline, including resource limitation through a saturating function of cor 
 availability and environmental modifiers affecting outbreak triggers.                                                                 
                                                                                                                                       
 However, the recovery dynamics for fast-growing and slow-growing corals are modeled independently using (100 - individual coral cover 
 which neglects the competitive interaction for limited substrate. In reality, both coral types compete for space; as one occupies mor 
 space, the recovery potential for the other is reduced.                                                                               
                                                                                                                                       
 Ecological Justification:                                                                                                             
 - Incorporates competition for substrate among corals.                                                                                
 - Reflects the limited available reef area, enhancing the realism of coral recovery dynamics during and after outbreaks.              
 - Provides a more integrated mechanism, ensuring that total coral cover does not exceed the maximum available space.                  
                                                                                                                                       
 This change improves ecological realism without adding new parameters.
