Below is our plan for an ecological improvement. In our assessment we found that although the model captures boom‐bust cycles, the     
resource limitation mechanism is oversimplified. Currently it uses a quadratic saturating function to account for coral availability in
fueling COTS growth. However, ecologically a Hill function is more appropriate to represent the nonlinear, saturating response of COTS 
to resource (coral) availability. In other words, below a threshold coral cover the uptake is strongly limited, and above that         
threshold the growth response saturates. This added nonlinearity (with a steepness exponent and a half‐saturation constant) better     
reflects the underlying dynamics and can improve the timing and magnitude predictions of outbreaks.
