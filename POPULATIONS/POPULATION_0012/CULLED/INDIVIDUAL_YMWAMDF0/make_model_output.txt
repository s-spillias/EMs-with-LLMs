Assessment & Reasoning:                                                                                                                                                                                                
The current model captures outbreak timing via a logistic growth that linearly reduces growth as COTS approach carrying capacity and a separate outbreak trigger function. However, the residuals indicate systematic  
misfits suggesting that a simple linear limitation may under‐represent the complexity of resource competition. In coral reef ecosystems, resource competition (e.g., for space and food) can induce nonlinear effects  
on population growth. Adding a new parameter—resource_limitation_exponent—allows the logistic term to be flexibly adjusted. With an exponent of 1, the model is unchanged. Values >1 increase the strength of          
limitation (a more rapid slow-down near carrying capacity), while values <1 soften the effect, potentially yielding a more realistic burst in growth when resources are plentiful. This added nonlinearity can thus    
help better capture the boom‐bust dynamics seen in episodic COTS outbreaks.                                                                                                                                            

The following changes are proposed:
