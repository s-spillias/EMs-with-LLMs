Summary of our assessment and chosen improvement:                                                                                      
The current model fits data moderately well, but residuals indicate that the outbreak trigger is sometimes too sensitive to short‐term 
fluctuations. To better capture the ecological inertia—that is, the delayed response of COTS outbreaks to past population levels—we    
introduce a new parameter, memory_effect. By blending (via a weighted average) the previous two time points of COTS population, the    
outbreak trigger becomes less “jumpy.” This modification creates a smoother, delayed outbreak response that more accurately represents 
the ecological processes at play.
