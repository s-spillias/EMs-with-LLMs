Assessment and Chosen Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                     
The current model reasonably captures the outbreak (boom-bust) dynamics via logistic growth and a smooth outbreak-trigger function. However, it oversimplifies the resource limitation mechanism since it does not     
account for how prey (coral) availability might constrain COTS growth. Ecologically, when coral cover (both fast‐ and slow-growing) declines, food becomes scarce, which should reduce the effective COTS growth rate. 
To capture this feedback, we introduce a new parameter, coral_effect, and modify the COTS growth equation to include a term that scales growth by the combined coral cover (normalized by the maximum possible, 200).  
This adjustment reflects the idea that abundant coral enhances growth, whereas scarce coral limits it.
