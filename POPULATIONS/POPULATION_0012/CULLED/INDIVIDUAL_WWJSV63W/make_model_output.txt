Assessment & Chosen Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                        
The current model captures the basic outbreak dynamics and coral recovery well but uses a constant coral predation efficiency. Our assessment from the residuals indicates that the model may under‐estimate the       
decline in coral cover at high COTS densities. Ecologically, it is reasonable to assume that when COTS numbers become extremely high, interference (or saturation) occurs—reducing the per‐capita coral predation rate.
We therefore propose to modify the predation term by incorporating a saturating (Michaelis–Menten) response using a new parameter predation_half_sat. This parameter represents the COTS density at which predation    
efficiency is halved, thereby providing a more nuanced representation of the predation process.                                                                                                                        

The following changes are proposed:                                                                                                                                                                                    

 1 Update intention.txt to document the assessment and rationale.                                                                                                                                                      
 2 Update parameters.json to add the new parameter predation_half_sat with appropriate ecological justification.                                                                                                       
 3 Update model.cpp to (a) declare the new parameter and (b) modify the equations for the fast- and slow-growing coral dynamics so that the coral predation term now uses the saturating function.
