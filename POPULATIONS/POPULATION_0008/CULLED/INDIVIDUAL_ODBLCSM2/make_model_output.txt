 Assessment and Rationale:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
 The current model fits the observed boom‐bust cycles reasonably well but appears to overestimate the impact of COTS predation due to its linear formulation. In reality, predator consumption tends to saturate at high prey densities because of handling time limitations.                                                                                                          
                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
 To address this, we introduce a Holling Type II (saturating) functional response by incorporating handling time parameters for predation on both fast- and slow-growing coral. This change limits the effective predation at high COTS densities, producing more ecologically realistic dynamics, and improves the model’s ability to capture the timing, magnitude, and duration of  
 outbreaks.
