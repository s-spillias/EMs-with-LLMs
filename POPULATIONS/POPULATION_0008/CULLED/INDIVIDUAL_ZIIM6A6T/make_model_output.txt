Rationale:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
The current model has a constant efficiency term for converting predation effects into COTS growth. However, ecologically, when coral cover is low the predation efficiency should decline because there is less food available. To capture this resource limitation, we introduce a scaling factor based on the combined availability of fast- and slow-growing coral (normalized to a
maximum of 200). This new term modulates the efficiency in the COTS dynamics so that reduced coral cover leads to a lower conversion efficiency, better representing the boom-bust cycles.                                                                                                                                                                                             

We add a new parameter, coral_availability_weight, to allow calibration of the strength of this effect.
