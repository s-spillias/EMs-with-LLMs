1. Model Fit and Project Context                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

 • The model uses lognormal likelihoods for COTS, fast coral, and slow coral, which is appropriate for positive-valued ecological data.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
 • The process model includes:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
    • Outbreaks triggered by environmental forcing (larval input, temperature)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         
    • Logistic growth for COTS and both coral groups                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
    • COTS predation on coral, with selectivity and a Holling Type II (saturating) functional response                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 
    • Outbreak state amplifies COTS growth                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
    • Competition for space between coral groups                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
 • The model structure is biologically plausible and addresses the boom-bust dynamics of COTS, their selective predation, and environmental triggers for outbreaks.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    

2. Missing or Oversimplified Processes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 

 • The model currently assumes that COTS predation on coral is only a function of COTS abundance and coral availability, with a saturating response.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
 • However, it does not include any feedback from coral depletion to COTS population dynamics beyond the immediate effect on predation efficiency. In reality, severe coral depletion can lead to a delayed negative feedback on COTS recruitment and survival, potentially causing sharper busts after outbreaks.                                                                     
 • The model also assumes that COTS immigration (larval input) is exogenous and not affected by local coral cover, which may be reasonable for open populations but may miss local feedbacks.                                                                                                                                                                                          

3. Proposed Ecological Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     

 • Introduce a non-linear, resource-dependent feedback on COTS recruitment or survival, such that when coral cover (resource) drops below a threshold, COTS population growth is sharply reduced.                                                                                                                                                                                      
 • This can be implemented by multiplying the COTS growth term by a resource limitation function (e.g., a sigmoid or saturating function of total coral cover).                                                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • This mechanism is ecologically justified: COTS larvae and juveniles require sufficient coral for settlement and survival; when coral is depleted, COTS populations crash more rapidly and recovery is delayed.                                                                                                                                                                      

Mathematical Formulation:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              

 • Let total_coral = fast_pred(t-1) + slow_pred(t-1)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
 • Introduce a new parameter logit_coral_thresh (logit of the coral cover threshold for COTS recruitment limitation)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
 • Define coral_thresh = 1/(1+exp(-logit_coral_thresh)) * (K_fast + K_slow) (threshold as a fraction of total carrying capacity)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
 • Define a resource limitation function, e.g.:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        
    • resource_lim = 1/(1 + exp(-kappa * (total_coral - coral_thresh)))                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                
    • Where kappa is a steepness parameter (fixed, e.g., 10)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
 • Multiply the COTS growth term by resource_lim                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Benefits:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              

 • This adds a biologically realistic feedback: after outbreaks, if coral is depleted, COTS cannot maintain high populations, leading to sharper busts and more realistic recovery lags.                                                                                                                                                                                               
 • The added complexity is minimal (one parameter), but the ecological realism is greatly improved.
