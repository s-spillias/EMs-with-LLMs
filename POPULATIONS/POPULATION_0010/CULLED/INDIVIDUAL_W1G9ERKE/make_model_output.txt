Effectiveness for Project Context

Missing or Oversimplified Ecological Processes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      

 • The model assumes a fixed efficiency of COTS predation on coral, regardless of COTS density.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • In reality, at high COTS densities, interference or crowding may reduce per capita predation efficiency (i.e., a density-dependent functional response).

 • Justification: Introducing a variable efficiency term (e.g., a Holling Type II or III functional response) for COTS predation on coral will better capture the non-linear relationship between COTS density and coral loss, especially during outbreaks. This reflects interference or satiation at high COTS densities, which is ecologically realistic and supported by field     
   observations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
 • Mathematical Form: Modify the predation term to include a half-saturation constant (handling time or interference parameter), so predation saturates at high COTS densities.
