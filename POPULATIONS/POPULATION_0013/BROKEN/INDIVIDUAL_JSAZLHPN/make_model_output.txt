Here is my assessment and proposed improvement:                                                                                                                                                                        

Assessment:                                                                                                                                                                                                            

 1 Model Fit:                                                                                                                                                                                                          
   The model uses lognormal likelihoods for COTS, fast coral, and slow coral, and predicts time series using lagged values. However, without the residuals file, I cannot quantitatively assess fit, but the structure 
   is appropriate for time series fitting.                                                                                                                                                                             
 2 Addressing Project Context:                                                                                                                                                                                         
   The model captures COTS boom-bust cycles via logistic growth, resource limitation (coral thresholds), SST effects, and immigration. It includes selective predation on fast/slow coral and feedbacks via coral      
   cover. However, the only environmental modifier is SST on COTS growth, and the only feedback is direct predation.                                                                                                   
 3 Missing/Oversimplified Processes:                                                                                                                                                                                   
   A key ecological process that may be oversimplified is the handling of COTS mortality. Currently, mortality is density-independent, but in reality, COTS populations may experience density-dependent mortality due 
   to predation, disease, or intraspecific competition, especially at outbreak densities. This can strongly influence the collapse phase of outbreaks and the duration of booms.                                       

Chosen Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                    
Add density-dependent COTS mortality by making the mortality rate increase with COTS abundance. This is ecologically justified: at high densities, COTS are more susceptible to predation, disease, and resource       
depletion, which can trigger rapid declines after outbreaks. Mathematically, this can be implemented by adding a quadratic mortality term (i.e., mortality = m_cots * N + m2_cots * N^2), where m2_cots is a new       
parameter.                                                                                                                                                                                                             

Justification for Complexity:                                                                                                                                                                                          
This is a minimal but ecologically meaningful extension. It adds only one parameter, is mathematically simple, and directly targets the boom-bust dynamics central to the project context.
