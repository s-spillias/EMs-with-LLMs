Below is our proposed set of changes and the accompanying ecological rationale.                                                                                                                                        

Assessment and Chosen Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                      

The current model reasonably captures the broad seasonal trends of COTS and coral, but the linear predation term may overly dampen the sudden outbreaks observed in the data. In reality, predator efficiency often    
saturates at high prey densities (a Holling type II response). This saturation means that even as coral cover increases, the effective predation pressure on COTS cannot increase indefinitely. Therefore, we propose  
introducing a saturating predation function controlled by a new parameter, delta. This change is ecologically justified as it allows the model to exhibit more pronounced boom-bust cycles of COTS by capturing the    
nonlinear limitations on predation. Also, note that all predictions exclusively use previous time step values to avoid data leakage.
