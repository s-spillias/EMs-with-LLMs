Assessment and Chosen Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     
The current model uses a linear predation term that assumes the rate of coral loss is directly proportional to coral cover. However, ecological data often show that predation rates saturate when prey (coral) are abundant. To better capture the boom‐bust dynamics of Crown of Thorns starfish (COTS) and the corresponding impact on coral communities, we propose to introduce a 
saturating (non‐linear) predation effect by adding half‐saturation constants for both fast- and slow-growing corals. This ensures that when coral cover is high, the predation effect does not increase indefinitely.                                                                                                                                                                  

This change is documented in the updated intention file and is supported by the ecological literature on predator saturation. In the model code, we add two new parameters (log_half_sat_fast and log_half_sat_slow), convert them to their natural scales and update the functional responses used in the coral dynamics equations to use a Michaelis–Menten style (saturating)       
formulation.
