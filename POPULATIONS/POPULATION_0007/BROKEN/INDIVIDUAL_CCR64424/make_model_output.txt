 Assessment and Reasoning:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
 The current model uses a fixed environmental trigger for COTS outbreaks that is independent of coral resource availability. Based on our examination—including the residual patterns—and project context which emphasizes capturing the boom-bust cycles realistically, this approach likely oversimplifies the system dynamics.                                                      
 Our proposed improvement introduces a resource limitation mechanism whereby the outbreak trigger is modulated by the previous time step’s fast and slow coral cover (normalized by 200). This ecological feedback ensures that when coral cover is high, the trigger for an outbreak is amplified, better reflecting the dependency of outbreak events on available resources.        
 Additionally, we add the new parameter "coral_effect_on_outbreak" to scale this effect, thus allowing for more nuanced calibration against observed data.
