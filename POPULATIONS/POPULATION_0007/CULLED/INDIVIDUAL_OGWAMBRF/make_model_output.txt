Assessment and Chosen Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     
Based on the residuals and the current performance of the model, the boom‐bust cycles observed in COTS outbreaks are not fully captured. In particular, the model lacks a density‐dependent regulation mechanism that would represent intraspecific competition (or even cannibalism) acting as a natural brake on exponential population growth. This modification is ecologically    
justified because, as COTS numbers increase, competition for limited resources (and potential cannibalism) are likely to induce additional mortality, contributing to the decline phase following an outbreak. Consequently, we are adding a density‐dependent regulation term to the COTS dynamics by subtracting a quadratic term that uses a new parameter (log_competition). This  
additional parameter will be calibrated against data to better capture the outbreak dynamics.
