 3 Missing or Oversimplified Processes:

Currently, the carrying capacity for COTS is a saturating function of total coral cover, but the effect of coral composition (fast vs. slow coral) on COTS recruitment and outbreak dynamics is not explicitly modeled. COTS preferentially feed on fast-growing Acropora, and  
   outbreaks are often triggered when fast coral is abundant. The current model sums fast and slow coral for resource limitation, which may not capture the selective predation and outbreak triggers.                                                                                                                                                                                 

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  
Refine the resource limitation mechanism by making COTS carrying capacity more sensitive to the abundance of fast coral (Acropora), using a non-linear (Hill-type) function of fast coral cover.                                                                                                                                                                                       

 • This will allow the model to better capture the ecological reality that COTS outbreaks are more likely and more intense when fast coral is abundant, and that slow coral alone does not sustain large COTS populations.                                                                                                                                                             
 • This is a moderate increase in complexity, justified by the strong ecological evidence for selective predation and outbreak triggers.
