2. Effectiveness for Project Context                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   

 • The model structure is appropriate for capturing boom-bust COTS cycles and their impact on coral communities.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       
 • It includes key processes: COTS recruitment, mortality, predation on corals, coral growth, and environmental drivers.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               
 • However, the model may oversimplify the feedback between coral cover and COTS outbreaks. In reality, COTS outbreaks are often triggered and sustained by high coral cover (especially Acropora), but as coral is depleted, COTS recruitment and survival should decline more sharply, leading to rapid busts.                                                                       

3. Missing or Oversimplified Processes                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 

 • The current model uses a simple logistic resource limitation for COTS (1 - cots/K_cots), but does not explicitly link COTS recruitment/survival to coral prey availability.                                                                                                                                                                                                         
 • This may under-represent the feedback where COTS populations crash rapidly after depleting their preferred coral prey.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              
 • There is no explicit threshold or non-linear collapse in COTS recruitment/survival as coral cover declines.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         

4. Proposed Ecological Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     

Approach:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              
Add a saturating (Michaelis-Menten) dependence of COTS recruitment on available fast coral (Acropora) cover. This introduces a direct, non-linear feedback: when Acropora is abundant, COTS recruitment is high; as Acropora is depleted, COTS recruitment drops sharply, accelerating the bust phase. This is ecologically justified, as COTS larvae and juveniles depend on coral for
survival, and outbreaks collapse when prey is scarce.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  

Mathematical Form:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     
Modify COTS recruitment as:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            
recruit_cots = r_cots * cots_pred(t-1) * (1 - cots_pred(t-1)/K_cots) * temp_eff_cots * (fast_pred(t-1) / (h_cots_coral + fast_pred(t-1)))                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              

Where h_cots_coral is a new half-saturation parameter for the effect of Acropora on COTS recruitment.
