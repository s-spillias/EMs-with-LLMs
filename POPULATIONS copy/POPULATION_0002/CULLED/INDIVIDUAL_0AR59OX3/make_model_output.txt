 • Yes. To better capture episodic boom-bust dynamics and realistic outbreak timing, I propose adding a 1-year maturation delay for endogenous (food-driven) recruitment into the adult COTS population. This adds delayed density dependence, a classic

 2 Explanation of changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                               

Assessment of the current model:                                                                                                                                                                                                                        

 • Fit to data: Cannot fully evaluate without the residuals file. Based on structure, the current model likely produces smoother adult COTS dynamics with peaks too synchronized with same-year conditions because all food-driven recruitment          
   immediately enters the adult class. This can under-represent the observed lagged boom following good feeding conditions and can damp oscillations.                                                                                                   
 • Alignment with project context: The model includes key mechanisms (Type-III predation on corals, temperature-driven bleaching, resource-limited COTS growth via coral availability, Allee effect, and thermal performance). However, episodic        
   outbreaks typically arise from lagged processes (larval production, settlement, and maturation), which are currently missing.                                                                                                                        
 • Missing/oversimplified processes:                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
    • Time-to-maturity lag from larval/juvenile to adult COTS (months to a couple of years). Lack of this lag can suppress the boom/bust cycles and timing offsets.                                                                                     
    • Potential over-removal of coral when predation rates become very high (no cap beyond soft penalties), but that’s a separate issue and not changed here to keep one focused improvement.                                                           

Parameter review:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

 • Several parameters have narrow or fixed bounds (e.g., m_C ≈ fixed at 2.56; Topt_C with narrow 28–29 bounds). These may be justified, but they reduce flexibility. Not changing them here.                                                            
 • The main structural mismatch is immediate conversion of consumption-driven recruitment into adults. Literature on COTS life history supports a 1–2 year maturation delay, motivating the proposed lag.                                               

Proposed ecological improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                                        

 • Add a 1-year maturation delay for the endogenous, food-driven recruitment pathway. This introduces delayed density dependence via a “recruitment buffer” that converts to adults in the following year with survival mat_surv.                       
 • Immigration remains immediate because it is already an external adult input at the annual timescale.                                                                                                                                                 
 • This improvement is minimal in complexity but powerful in producing outbreak timing, amplitude, and duration consistent with observations.                                                                                                           

Mathematical changes:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   

 • Introduce a state variable rec_food_lag storing last year’s food-driven recruitment.                                                                                                                                                                 
 • Adult additions at year t: mat_surv * rec_food_lag + imm_to_adults (immediate immigration).                                                                                                                                                          
 • Update lag each step: rec_food_lag = rec_from_food.                                                                                                                                                                                                  
 • Initialize lag with a new parameter rec_lag_init to capture pre-model conditions.                                                                                                                                                                    
 • No data leakage: all process terms for year t are functions of state and drivers from t-1 only.
