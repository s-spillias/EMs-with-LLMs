Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                                                                                                 

The most significant gap is the lack of temperature dependence on biological rates. Temperature is a fundamental driver of metabolic rates in ectothermic organisms like plankton. The Arrhenius/Q10 relationship shows that
biological rates typically double for every 10°C increase. This is particularly important because:                                                                                                                          

 • Phytoplankton growth rates are highly temperature-sensitive                                                                                                                                                              
 • Zooplankton grazing rates increase with temperature                                                                                                                                                                      
 • The differential temperature sensitivity of phytoplankton vs. zooplankton can create match-mismatch dynamics                                                                                                             
 • Temperature interacts with light seasonality to drive bloom timing                                                                                                                                                       

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add temperature-dependent rate modifiers using the Arrhenius equation, which is the standard approach in marine biogeochemical models. This will allow:                                                                     

 1 More realistic seasonal dynamics when coupled with varying temperature                                                                                                                                                   
 2 Better representation of metabolic constraints                                                                                                                                                                           
 3 Improved predictions across different oceanic regions (tropical vs. temperate vs. polar)                                                                                                                                 

The mathematical form will use: rate_modifier = exp(A_E * (1/T_ref - 1/T)) where A_E is the activation energy scaled by the gas constant, T is temperature in Kelvin, and T_ref is a reference temperature.
