Key Ecological Process Missing: Temperature Dependence                                                                                                                                                                      

The most significant missing process is temperature dependence of biological rates. This is fundamental to plankton ecology because:                                                                                        

 • Phytoplankton growth rates typically double for every 10°C increase (Q10 ≈ 2)                                                                                                                                            
 • Zooplankton grazing and metabolism are even more temperature-sensitive (Q10 ≈ 2-3)                                                                                                                                       
 • Temperature affects the balance between autotrophs and heterotrophs                                                                                                                                                      
 • Seasonal temperature cycles drive bloom timing and succession                                                                                                                                                            


                                                                                Proposed Improvement: Temperature-Dependent Rate Modulation                                                                                 

I will add temperature dependence to all biological rate parameters using the Eppley (1972) formulation, which is the standard in marine ecosystem models:                                                                  

rate(T) = rate(T_ref) × exp(k_T × (T - T_ref))                                                                                                                                                                              

Where:                                                                                                                                                                                                                      

 • T_ref = reference temperature (typically 20°C)                                                                                                                                                                           
 • k_T = temperature coefficient (typically 0.063 for phytoplankton, based on Eppley 1972)                                                                                                                                  
 • Different k_T values for different processes reflect differential temperature sensitivity                                                                                                                                

This approach:                                                                                                                                                                                                              

 1 Is well-established in marine biogeochemistry                                                                                                                                                                            
 2 Adds minimal complexity (one parameter per process)                                                                                                                                                                      
 3 Enables seasonal dynamics and climate change scenarios                                                                                                                                                                   
 4 Maintains model tractability while adding realism
