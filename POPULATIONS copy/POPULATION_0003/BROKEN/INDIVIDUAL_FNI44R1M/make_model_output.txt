Yes. To better capture episodic boom–bust dynamics without data leakage, I’m adding an explicit juvenile stage that introduces a biologically realistic multi-year maturation delay. This delay is a well-supported mechanism for COTS outbreaks        
(cryptic juveniles that mature after 2–3 years), and it helps produce sharper, timed adult booms sustained by prior resource consumption pulses. No current-time data will be used in predictions.                                                      

 2 Explanation of the needed changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                    

 • Ecological rationale: The current model moves recruits directly into adults in the next time step (via sJ), which tends to smooth dynamics and can mis-time outbreaks. COTS juveniles often remain cryptic for multiple years; a juvenile pool with  
   survival and maturation provides delayed density dependence and sharper boom-bust cycles consistent with the project context.                                                                                                                        
 • Structural change:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   
    • Add a juvenile state J_t (ind/m2).                                                                                                                                                                                                                
    • Reinterpret sJ as settlement/early survival into the juvenile pool (not directly to adults).                                                                                                                                                      
    • Add juvenile annual mortality mJ and maturation rate gJ (fraction/year).                                                                                                                                                                          
    • Adults at t+1 receive matured juveniles from the previous juvenile pool: A_{t+1} = BH((1-mC0) A_t + gJ (1-mJ) J_t) with Beverton–Holt crowding.                                                                                                   
    • Juveniles update as: J_{t+1} = softplus((1-gJ)(1-mJ) J_t + sJ R_t).                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Parameters.json updates:                                                                                                                                                                                                                             
    • Update sJ description to reflect settlement/early survival into juvenile pool.                                                                                                                                                                    
    • Add mJ and gJ parameters with bounds [0,1].
