I have reviewed the current model and propose one meaningful ecological improvement that better captures episodic COTS outbreaks without introducing data leakage or new data requirements.                                                             

Assessment summary:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     

 • Fit to data: The model is structurally sound and likely fits coral trends reasonably under logit-normal observation models. However, the single-step pathway from recruits to adults may smooth cohort dynamics and misalign outbreak timing and     
   magnitude.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           
 • Alignment with project context: Outbreak triggers and boom-bust timing are only partially addressed. The model includes thermal performance, Allee effects, predation feedback, and immigration, but lacks multi-year juvenile lags known to drive   
   cohort resonance and episodic dynamics in COTS.                                                                                                                                                                                                      
 • Missing/oversimplified processes: A key omission is explicit juvenile stage structure causing 2+ year delays between larval production/survival and peak adult densities. This delay is critical to forming the sharp outbreak pulses observed on the
   GBR.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 

Chosen improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     

 • Add a two-stage juvenile pipeline (J1, J2) with stage-specific survival sJ1 and sJ2, so that adults at t+1 are produced from J2 at t. This introduces a biologically plausible 2-year delay, enhances the capacity for boom-bust cycles, and         
   preserves stability via the existing Beverton–Holt crowding on adults.                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Replace the single sJ parameter with sJ1 and sJ2 (both in [0,1]). No new data are needed.

 • R_t is unchanged, still driven by thermal performance, food-fueled reproduction, Allee effects, and immigration (using only previous time-step states and forcings).
