                                                                                            Model Assessment and Proposed Change                                                                                            

 1 Model Fitness and Project Context:                                                                                                                                                                                       
   The current model provides a solid foundation, using a standard Lotka-Volterra competition model for corals and a Holling Type II functional response for COTS predation. This structure captures the fundamental        
   interactions between COTS and their coral prey. However, the project context emphasizes modeling episodic outbreaks and the characteristic boom-bust cycles. The current COTS population model (dC/dt = C * (growth -    
   mortality) + immigration) is somewhat simplified. Growth is directly proportional to food intake, and mortality is a linear function of density. While larval immigration is included, it's an external data driver      
   (cotsimm_dat) rather than a dynamic part of the system's reproduction. A key mechanism missing is the reproductive dynamics that can lead to population explosions from low-density states.                              
 2 Missing Ecological Processes:                                                                                                                                                                                            
   COTS are broadcast spawners, meaning their reproductive success is highly dependent on population density to ensure fertilization. At low densities, per-capita reproductive success is very low (an Allee effect). This 
   process is crucial for explaining why COTS populations can remain at low, stable levels for long periods and then suddenly erupt when a density threshold is crossed. The current model does not capture this non-linear 
   reproductive success.                                                                                                                                                                                                    
 3 Proposed Improvement: Incorporate a Component Allee Effect:                                                                                                                                                              
   I will introduce a component Allee effect into the COTS growth equation. This will make the per-capita growth rate dependent on COTS density, suppressing growth at low numbers and allowing for rapid, non-linear       
   expansion once a density threshold is surpassed. This provides an intrinsic mechanism for the boom-bust dynamics central to the project.                                                                                 
   The change involves modifying the COTS growth term to include a saturating function dependent on COTS density:                                                                                                           
   cots_growth = e_c * food_intake * (C / (C + allee_A))                                                                                                                                                                    
   where C is the COTS density and allee_A is a new parameter representing the Allee thresholdâ€”the density at which reproductive success is half its maximum.                                                               

This change requires adding one new parameter, allee_A, to parameters.json and updating the COTS growth calculation in model.cpp. I will also document this rationale in intention.txt.
