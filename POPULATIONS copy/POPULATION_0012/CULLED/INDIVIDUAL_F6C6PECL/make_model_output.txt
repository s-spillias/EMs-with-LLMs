Current Model Strengths:                                                                                                                        

 1 Includes density-dependent fecundity enhancement (Hill function) - captures outbreak self-amplification                                      
 2 Nutrient-mediated larval survival enhancement - captures environmental triggers                                                              
 3 Allee effects for low-density population dynamics                                                                                            
 4 Type II functional responses for coral predation                                                                                             
 5 Temperature effects on both COTS and coral                                                                                                   

Potential Issues:                                                                                                                               

 1 The model has TWO separate mechanisms enhancing COTS recruitment (nutrient enhancement AND density-dependent fecundity), which may be        
   redundant or create unrealistic positive feedbacks                                                                                           
 2 The density-dependent fecundity operates on immigration (external larvae), which is ecologically questionable - fecundity should affect local
   reproduction, not immigrant survival                                                                                                         
 3 Missing explicit local reproduction term - the model only has immigration, no endogenous larval production                                   
 4 The "nutrient effect" using SST anomaly as a proxy is indirect and may confound temperature effects                                          

Key Missing Process:                                                                                                                            

The model lacks explicit local larval production from the resident COTS population. Currently, all recruitment comes from external immigration  
modified by various factors. Real COTS outbreaks involve both:                                                                                  

 • Primary outbreaks (triggered by favorable conditions enhancing external larval settlement)                                                   
 • Secondary outbreaks (self-seeding from local reproduction during high-density phases)                                                        


                                                              Proposed Improvement                                                              

Add explicit local reproduction with density-dependent fertilization success, while simplifying the immigration pathway. This addresses the     
PROJECT CONTEXT emphasis on "mechanisms that trigger and sustain outbreak events."                                                              

Ecological Justification:                                                                                                                       

 • COTS are broadcast spawners - fertilization success increases dramatically with density (Allee effect at reproduction level)                 
 • During outbreaks, local reproduction can sustain populations even when immigration declines                                                  
 • This creates a more realistic boom-bust mechanism: immigration triggers initial increase → high density enables successful local reproduction
   → outbreak sustains itself → food depletion causes crash                                                                                     

Mathematical Approach:                                                                                                                          

Replace the current fecundity enhancement of immigration with a separate local reproduction term that has strong density-dependent fertilization
success (Hill function).
