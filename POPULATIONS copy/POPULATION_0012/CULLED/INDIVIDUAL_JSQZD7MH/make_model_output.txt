                                                           Current Model Performance                                                            

The model implements a comprehensive COTS-coral system with:                                                                                    

 • Temperature-dependent recruitment with Gaussian response                                                                                     
 • Nutrient-mediated larval survival with sigmoidal threshold                                                                                   
 • Age-structured mortality (juvenile vs adult)                                                                                                 
 • Allee effects, density dependence, and food limitation                                                                                       
 • Type II functional responses for coral predation                                                                                             
 • Logistic coral growth                                                                                                                        

                                                             Key Issues Identified                                                              

 1 Missing Coral Recovery Mechanism: The model lacks representation of how coral communities recover after COTS outbreaks decline. In reality,  
   there's often competitive release where fast-growing Acropora can rapidly recolonize after slow-growing corals are depleted, creating        
   temporal niche partitioning.                                                                                                                 
 2 Oversimplified Competition: The current carrying capacities are independent, but fast and slow corals compete for the same benthic space.    
   When one coral type is depleted by COTS, the other should have enhanced growth potential.                                                    
 3 Parameter Values Suggest Structural Issues:                                                                                                  
    • log_conversion_eff = -1.5 (conversion_eff ≈ 0.22) is quite high for coral-to-COTS biomass conversion                                      
    • The growth-from-feeding term may be overestimating COTS population growth                                                                 
    • Attack rates show strong preference (fast: 1.65, slow: 0.61), but this isn't modulated by coral availability                              

                                           Proposed Improvement: Dynamic Competitive Space Allocation                                           

I'll implement competitive space dynamics where the effective carrying capacity for each coral type depends on the abundance of the other. This 
captures:                                                                                                                                       

 • When COTS preferentially consume Acropora (fast corals), massive corals (slow) can expand into freed space                                   
 • After outbreak decline, fast corals can rapidly recolonize                                                                                   
 • This creates realistic boom-bust-recovery cycles                                                                                             

This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                         

 1 Corals compete for limited benthic substrate                                                                                                 
 2 COTS selective predation creates competitive release                                                                                         
 3 Fast corals have higher recruitment but are preferentially eaten                                                                             
 4 This mechanism is critical for understanding multi-decadal outbreak cycles

 +=== ECOLOGICAL IMPROVEMENT: COMPETITIVE SPACE DYNAMICS ===                                                                                    
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +Rationale:                                                                                                                                    
 +The Great Barrier Reef benthic substrate is a limited resource. When COTS preferentially consume fast-growing Acropora, this creates          
 competitive release for slow-growing massive corals to expand. Conversely, after COTS outbreaks decline, fast corals can rapidly recolonize    
 freed space. This dynamic is critical for:                                                                                                     
 +1. Realistic boom-bust-recovery cycles                                                                                                        
 +2. Community composition shifts during/after outbreaks                                                                                        
 +3. Multi-decadal outbreak periodicity                                                                                                         
 +4. Explaining why reefs don't permanently shift to slow-coral dominance                                                                       
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +Mathematical Implementation:                                                                                                                  
 +Replace fixed carrying capacities with dynamic effective carrying capacities that depend on space occupancy:                                  
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +K_fast_effective = K_fast_max * (1 - slow_coral / total_space)                                                                                
 +K_slow_effective = K_slow_max * (1 - fast_coral / total_space)                                                                                
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +Where total_space represents maximum benthic cover (typically 80-90% for healthy reefs).                                                      
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +This creates:                                                                                                                                 
 +- Competitive exclusion pressure when both corals are abundant                                                                                
 +- Competitive release when one type is depleted by COTS                                                                                       
 +- Realistic space limitation without requiring explicit competition coefficients                                                              
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +New Parameters:                                                                                                                               
 +- total_benthic_space: Maximum total coral cover (% cover), typically 80-90%                                                                  
 +- competition_strength: Modulates how strongly each coral type inhibits the other (0-1 scale)                                                 
 +
