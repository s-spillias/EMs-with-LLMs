                                                           Current Model Performance                                                            

Without seeing the residuals file, I can analyze the model structure:                                                                           

Strengths:                                                                                                                                      

 1 The model includes sophisticated recruitment dynamics with accumulated potential and threshold-based recruitment                             
 2 Age-structured mortality (juvenile vs. adult) is implemented                                                                                 
 3 Prey-switching behavior between fast and slow corals is modeled                                                                              
 4 Multiple environmental drivers (SST, nutrient proxy) affect recruitment                                                                      

Potential Issues:                                                                                                                               

 1 Recruitment mechanism may be overly complex: The model has both a recruitment potential accumulation system AND a nutrient-mediated larval   
   survival threshold. These two non-linear mechanisms may be redundant or competing.                                                           
 2 Missing key outbreak trigger: Literature on COTS outbreaks emphasizes that phytoplankton blooms driven by terrestrial runoff are a primary   
   driver of larval survival. The current model uses SST as a proxy for nutrient conditions, but this is indirect. The bloom optimal temperature
   (26.5°C) being cooler than recruitment optimal (28.5°C) attempts to capture wet season dynamics, but this is mechanistically weak.           
 3 Larval survival base parameter has suspicious value: log_larval_survival_base = 0.0 means larval_survival_base = 1.0. Combined with the      
   sigmoidal nutrient response, this suggests larval survival can reach very high values (potentially >1.0 as a multiplier), which may be       
   unrealistic.                                                                                                                                 
 4 Food limitation is additive to mortality: The current formulation adds food limitation directly to mortality rate, but COTS can survive      
   extended periods without food. A more realistic mechanism would be reduced growth/fecundity rather than increased mortality.                 

                                                         Key Ecological Process Missing                                                         

The most critical missing element is explicit representation of the link between environmental conditions and food availability for larvae. COTS
outbreaks are strongly associated with:                                                                                                         

 • Flood plumes delivering nutrients                                                                                                            
 • Resulting phytoplankton blooms                                                                                                               
 • Enhanced larval survival through the critical 2-4 week planktonic phase                                                                      

The current model conflates this with temperature effects, which is mechanistically incorrect.                                                  


                                                              Proposed Improvement                                                              

Add a dynamic larval food availability state variable that responds to environmental conditions and directly affects recruitment success. This  
will:                                                                                                                                           

 1 Replace the indirect SST-based nutrient proxy with an explicit food availability pool                                                        
 2 Separate temperature effects (which affect development rate and survival) from food effects (which affect growth and survival)               
 3 Create a more mechanistically realistic outbreak trigger                                                                                     
 4 Simplify the recruitment pathway by removing the redundant nutrient threshold mechanism                                                      

This approach maintains model complexity at a similar level but improves ecological realism by explicitly representing the phytoplankton bloom →
larval food → recruitment pathway that drives COTS outbreaks.
