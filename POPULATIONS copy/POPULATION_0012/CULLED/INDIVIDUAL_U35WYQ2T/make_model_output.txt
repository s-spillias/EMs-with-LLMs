                                                          1. Current Model Evaluation                                                           

The model includes:

 • Temperature-dependent recruitment                                                                                                            
 • Phytoplankton bloom dynamics affecting larval survival                                                                                       
 • Type II functional response for coral predation

 • Allee effects and density dependence                                                                                                         

                                                           2. Fit to PROJECT CONTEXT                                                            

The model addresses boom-bust cycles through:                                                                                                   

 • Recruitment pulses (immigration + temperature + bloom effects)                                                                               
 • Age-structured mortality                                                                                                                     
 • Density dependence                                                                                                                           

However, a critical gap exists: The model lacks a mechanism for outbreak self-limitation through coral depletion. In real COTS outbreaks,       
populations crash not just from intrinsic mortality, but because they consume their food supply to critically low levels, triggering starvation 
and emigration.                                                                                                                                 

                                                             3. Key Missing Process                                                             

Food-dependent reproduction: Current recruitment is driven entirely by external immigration and environmental conditions, with no feedback from 
local adult population size or food availability. Real COTS outbreaks are sustained by local reproduction when food is abundant, but this       
reproduction collapses when coral is depleted. This is THE key mechanism for boom-bust dynamics.                                                

                                                              4. Parameter Review                                                               

Looking at temp_optimal (28.5°C with tight bounds 28.0-29.0°C from literature), the current Gaussian temperature response may be too            
restrictive. COTS can recruit across a broader temperature range, with optimal conditions being more of a plateau than a sharp peak.            


                                                 Proposed Improvement: Food-Dependent Fecundity                                                 

I will add local reproduction with food-dependent fecundity to capture the boom-bust mechanism. This adds:                                      

 1 Adult fecundity parameter: Eggs per adult per year (literature: ~10-60 million eggs/female)                                                  
 2 Food-dependent fecundity modifier: Reproduction scales with coral availability (well-fed adults produce more viable gametes)                 
 3 Fertilization success: Density-dependent fertilization (requires sufficient adult density)                                                   
 4 Local larval retention: Fraction of locally-produced larvae that recruit locally                                                             

This creates the critical feedback: abundant coral → high fecundity → population growth → coral depletion → low fecundity → population crash.
