                                                                   Assessment                                                                   

Current Model Performance:                                                                                                                      

The model captures COTS-coral dynamics with temperature-dependent recruitment, nutrient-mediated larval survival, age-structured mortality, and 
Type II functional responses for predation. However, examining the structure reveals a potential issue with the outbreak triggering mechanism.  

Key Observation:                                                                                                                                

The nutrient-mediated larval survival mechanism (Equations 2-3) uses a sigmoidal threshold response, but the current parameter values suggest   
this may not be calibrated optimally:                                                                                                           

 • sst_bloom_optimal = 26.5°C (cooler, wet season conditions)                                                                                   
 • nutrient_threshold = 0.5 (midpoint threshold)                                                                                                
 • log_nutrient_steepness = 1.0 (moderate steepness)                                                                                            

Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                     

The model lacks coral community composition feedback on COTS recruitment success. In reality, COTS settlement and early juvenile survival are   
strongly influenced by the availability of crustose coralline algae (CCA) and coral rubble structure. When coral cover is high and diverse, it  
provides better settlement habitat and refugia for juvenile COTS, creating a positive feedback that can amplify outbreaks. Conversely, degraded 
reefs with low coral cover provide poor settlement conditions.                                                                                  

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                           

Add a habitat quality modifier to recruitment that depends on total coral cover. This creates an ecologically realistic feedback where:         

 1 Healthy reefs with abundant coral provide better settlement substrate → higher recruitment success                                           
 2 Degraded reefs with low coral cover → reduced recruitment success                                                                            
 3 This creates a threshold dynamic where outbreaks are more likely on healthy reefs (paradoxically)                                            
 4 After severe coral depletion, recruitment drops, helping terminate outbreaks                                                                 

This addresses the PROJECT CONTEXT by providing an additional mechanism that influences outbreak timing and duration through reef habitat       
quality.

 +- Protection from predators in complex reef structure

 +When coral cover is high and diverse, the reef provides:                                                                                      
 +- More settlement substrate (CCA grows better on healthy reefs)                                                                               
 +- Better refugia for juveniles (complex 3D structure)                                                                                         
 +- Higher food availability for early juveniles (biofilm on coral surfaces)                                                                    
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +When coral cover is depleted:                                                                                                                 
 +- Settlement substrate degrades                                                                                                               
 +- Structural complexity collapses                                                                                                             
 +- Juvenile survival plummets                                                                                                                  
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +MATHEMATICAL IMPLEMENTATION:                                                                                                                  
 +Add a saturating function of total coral cover that modifies recruitment:                                                                     
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +habitat_quality = (total_coral^habitat_exponent) / (habitat_halfsat^habitat_exponent + total_coral^habitat_exponent)                          
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +This creates:                                                                                                                                 
 +- Low recruitment when coral cover is very low (degraded reef)                                                                                
 +- Increasing recruitment as coral cover increases (better habitat)                                                                            
 +- Saturation at high coral cover (diminishing returns)                                                                                        
 +- Positive feedback: healthy reefs → better recruitment → potential outbreaks                                                                 
 +- Negative feedback: severe depletion → poor recruitment → outbreak termination                                                               
 +                                                                                                                                              
 +PARAMETERS ADDED:                                                                                                                             
 +1. log_habitat_effect: Strength of habitat quality effect on recruitment                                                                      
 +2. habitat_halfsat: Coral cover at which habitat quality reaches 50% of maximum                                                               
 +3. habitat_exponent: Steepness of habitat quality response (Hill coefficient)

 +- Natural outbreak termination when coral depletion degrades habitat
