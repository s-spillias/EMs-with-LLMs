                                                         3. Missing Ecological Process

Ecological reality: COTS larvae are more tolerant of temperatures below optimal than above. Cooler water slows development but doesn't kill     
larvae, while warmer water causes thermal stress and mortality. This asymmetry is crucial for outbreak dynamics because it means recruitment can
occur over a broader temperature range during cooler periods.                                                                                   

                                            4. Proposed Improvement: Asymmetric Temperature Response

 Type temp_response = exp(-temp_effect * temp_deviation * temp_deviation);

 • Has gentler decline below optimal temperature (larvae survive but develop slower)                                                            
 • Has steeper decline above optimal temperature (thermal stress mortality)                                                                     
 • Better captures the 28.0-29.0°C optimal range from literature                                                                                

This single change will:                                                                                                                        

 1 Allow recruitment during sub-optimal but non-lethal cooler periods                                                                           
 2 Maintain strong recruitment during optimal windows                                                                                           
 3 Prevent recruitment during thermal stress events                                                                                             
 4 Create more realistic outbreak initiation conditions
