                                                            Current Model Evaluation                                                            

Strengths:

 2 Allee effects are implemented (Equation 2) to capture low-density population dynamics                                                        
 3 Nutrient-mediated larval survival enhancement (Equation 1b) provides environmental triggering

Weaknesses:                                                                                                                                     

 1 Missing larval development time-lag: COTS larvae take several weeks to develop. Current recruitment (Equation 10) uses same-timestep         
   environmental conditions, which may not capture the temporal mismatch between spawning conditions and settlement                             
 2 Oversimplified temperature-fecundity relationship: The model assumes temperature only affects larval survival, but adult COTS spawning       
   success and timing are also temperature-dependent                                                                                            
 3 No explicit starvation mortality: During outbreak collapse, COTS deplete coral resources but the model only has density-dependent mortality,

 • log_max_fecundity_boost = 1.609 (5x enhancement) - substantial effect                                                                        
 • fecundity_halfsat = exp(-0.693) = 0.5 ind/m² - relatively low threshold                                                                      

The density-dependent fecundity mechanism appears to be the primary outbreak driver, but it may need refinement.                                

                                                              Proposed Improvement                                                              

Add resource-dependent COTS mortality during coral depletion phases                                                                             

This addresses a critical gap: during outbreak collapse, COTS experience starvation as they deplete coral resources. The current model only has 
baseline and density-dependent mortality, missing the feedback where resource depletion accelerates population crash.                           

Ecological justification:                                                                                                                       

 • COTS are obligate coral predators - they cannot switch to alternative food sources                                                           
 • During late outbreak phases, coral cover drops below levels that can sustain the COTS population                                             
 • Starvation mortality creates the characteristic rapid "bust" phase                                                                           
 • This mechanism is essential for capturing the asymmetric boom-bust dynamics emphasized in the PROJECT CONTEXT                                

Mathematical approach:                                                                                                                          

Add a starvation mortality term that increases as per-capita coral availability falls below a critical threshold, using a reverse Hill function 
to create a sharp transition.
