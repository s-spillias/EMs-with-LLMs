                                                    Model Evaluation Against PROJECT CONTEXT                                                    

The model addresses several key outbreak mechanisms:                                                                                            

 1 Recruitment dynamics: Immigration + local spawning with temperature and nutrient effects                                                     
 2 Age structure: Juvenile vs. adult mortality with maturation dynamics                                                                         
 3 Outbreak triggers: Nutrient-mediated larval survival with threshold response                                                                 
 4 Coral predation: Type II functional response with preferential feeding                                                                       

                                                        Missing/Oversimplified Processes                                                        

A critical gap: The model lacks size-dependent predation efficiency. In reality, COTS feeding rates and coral preferences change dramatically   
with body size. Juveniles (<15cm) feed primarily on algae and small coral polyps, while adults (15-35cm) are the primary coral predators. Large 
adults (>35cm) can consume coral at 2-3x the rate of newly mature individuals. This size-structured predation is crucial for capturing outbreak 
dynamics because:                                                                                                                               

 1 Delayed impact: There's a lag between recruitment pulses and coral damage                                                                    
 2 Accelerating damage: As COTS grow, coral consumption accelerates non-linearly                                                                
 3 Outbreak persistence: Large individuals sustain outbreaks even as recruitment declines                                                       

                                           Proposed Improvement: Size-Structured Predation Efficiency                                           

I'll add a body size state variable that tracks the average size of the COTS population, with size-dependent attack rates. This captures the    
ecological reality that outbreak severity depends not just on COTS numbers, but on their size distribution.                                     

Ecological justification:                                                                                                                       

 • Small juveniles (0-2 years): Minimal coral predation, primarily algae feeders                                                                
 • Medium adults (2-4 years): Moderate coral predation, newly reproductive                                                                      
 • Large adults (4+ years): Maximum coral predation, highly fecund                                                                              

This creates a more realistic outbreak trajectory: slow buildup → explosive damage phase → gradual decline.
