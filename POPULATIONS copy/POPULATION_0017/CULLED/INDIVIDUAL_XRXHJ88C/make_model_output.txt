Current Model Structure:                                                                                                                                                                                                    

The model simulates nutrient-phytoplankton-zooplankton (NPZ) dynamics with:                                                                                                                                                 

 • Nutrient uptake by phytoplankton (Michaelis-Menten with light co-limitation)                                                                                                                                             
 • Zooplankton grazing (Holling Type II)                                                                                                                                                                                    
 • Mixed linear-quadratic phytoplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                                           
 • Nutrient recycling from mortality                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Light limitation with self-shading                                                                                                                                                                                       

Key Observation:                                                                                                                                                                                                            

The model includes light limitation but treats it as a simple co-limiting factor. However, in real oceanic systems, phytoplankton photoacclimation (physiological adjustment to light conditions) is a critical process.    
Phytoplankton adjust their cellular chlorophyll content and photosynthetic efficiency based on ambient light conditions, which affects both their growth rate and their light attenuation properties.                       

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add photoacclimation dynamics - a state variable for phytoplankton chlorophyll-to-carbon ratio (θ) that adjusts dynamically based on light availability. This captures:                                                     

 1 Phytoplankton increase chlorophyll content in low light to maximize light capture                                                                                                                                        
 2 Phytoplankton decrease chlorophyll content in high light to avoid photodamage                                                                                                                                            
 3 Chlorophyll content affects light attenuation (self-shading feedback)                                                                                                                                                    
 4 This creates realistic bloom dynamics and depth-dependent productivity                                                                                                                                                   

This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                                                                                                     

 • Photoacclimation is a fundamental phytoplankton response observed across all taxa                                                                                                                                        
 • It explains why phytoplankton can maintain growth across wide light gradients                                                                                                                                            
 • It creates important feedbacks in bloom dynamics                                                                                                                                                                         
 • It's a well-documented process with established mathematical formulations

 +- Decreases when light is saturating (to avoid photodamage and reduce metabolic costs)
