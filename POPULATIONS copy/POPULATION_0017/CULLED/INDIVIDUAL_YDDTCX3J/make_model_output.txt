Current Model Strengths:                                                                                                                                                                                                    

 1 Implements NPZ (Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton) dynamics with nutrient recycling                                                                                                                                     
 2 Includes light limitation with self-shading effects

Potential Issues:                                                                                                                                                                                                           

 1 Temperature effects are absent - Ocean temperature strongly affects metabolic rates (growth, grazing, mortality)                                                                                                         
 2 Light limitation uses a simple Monod form - doesn't capture photoinhibition at high light                                                                                                                                
 3 Zooplankton mortality is quadratic in Z only - doesn't account for higher predation                                                                                                                                      
 4 No seasonal forcing despite oceanic systems showing strong seasonality                                                                                                                                                   

Key Ecological Gap:                                                                                                                                                                                                         

The model lacks temperature dependence, which is critical for plankton dynamics. All biological rates (r, g_max, m_P, m_Z) are temperature-sensitive following Q10 or Arrhenius relationships. Ocean temperature varies     
seasonally and with depth, fundamentally driving plankton phenology and productivity.                                                                                                                                       


                                                                                Proposed Improvement: Temperature-Dependent Metabolic Rates                                                                                 

I'll add temperature as a forcing variable and implement Q10-based temperature dependence for all biological rate parameters. This is ecologically justified because:                                                       

 1 Phytoplankton growth increases exponentially with temperature (Q10 ≈ 1.5-2.5)                                                                                                                                            
 2 Zooplankton grazing is highly temperature-sensitive (Q10 ≈ 2-3)                                                                                                                                                          
 3 Mortality rates also increase with temperature due to higher metabolic costs                                                                                                                                             
 4 This allows the model to capture seasonal dynamics and climate change effects                                                                                                                                            

The Q10 formulation: rate(T) = rate(T_ref) * Q10^((T - T_ref)/10)                                                                                                                                                           

This is simpler than full Arrhenius but captures the essential temperature dependence with clear biological interpretation.
