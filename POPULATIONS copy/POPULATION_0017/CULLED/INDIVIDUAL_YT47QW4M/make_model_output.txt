 • Includes nutrient limitation (Michaelis-Menten)                                                                                                                                                                          
 • Light limitation with self-shading                                                                                                                                                                                       
 • Temperature dependence (Q10 formulation)

Key Observation from Parameters:                                                                                                                                                                                            

The zooplankton mortality coefficient m_Z has been updated from literature with a very specific value (2.426) and tight bounds (2.276-2.576), suggesting this is an important calibrated parameter. However, the current    
quadratic mortality formulation may be oversimplified for representing the complex predation and mortality processes affecting zooplankton.                                                                                 

Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                                                                                                 

A critical missing component is zooplankton metabolic costs (basal respiration/excretion). Currently, zooplankton only lose biomass through quadratic mortality, but in reality, they have continuous metabolic demands     
that:                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

 1 Scale with biomass (not quadratic)                                                                                                                                                                                       
 2 Are temperature-dependent                                                                                                                                                                                                
 3 Return nutrients to the system                                                                                                                                                                                           
 4 Represent a significant energy drain independent of predation                                                                                                                                                            

This is particularly important because:                                                                                                                                                                                     

 • The assimilation efficiency e = 0.3 means 70% of grazed material is already lost                                                                                                                                         
 • But there's no ongoing metabolic cost for maintaining zooplankton biomass                                                                                                                                                
 • This can lead to unrealistic zooplankton accumulation in nutrient-rich conditions                                                                                                                                        

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add a linear zooplankton respiration/excretion term that represents basal metabolic costs. This will:                                                                                                                       

 • Provide a density-independent mortality pathway                                                                                                                                                                          
 • Add temperature-sensitive metabolic costs                                                                                                                                                                                
 • Create an additional nutrient recycling pathway                                                                                                                                                                          
 • Better represent the energetic constraints on zooplankton populations                                                                                                                                                    

This is a well-established process in marine ecosystem models (e.g., NPZD models) and fills a clear gap in the current formulation.
