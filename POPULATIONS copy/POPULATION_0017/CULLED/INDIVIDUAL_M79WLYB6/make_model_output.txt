                                                                                              Key Observation from Parameters                                                                                               

The parameter r (phytoplankton growth rate) has been updated from literature with a value of 1.0 day^-1 and bounds [0.8, 1.2]. This is relatively high and suggests productive conditions. However, the model currently uses
a simple Michaelis-Menten formulation for nutrient limitation that may not capture the full complexity of nutrient uptake dynamics, especially under varying light conditions.                                              

                                                                                                       Identified Gap                                                                                                       

The current model treats light and nutrient limitation as simple multiplicative factors. In reality, phytoplankton exhibit photoacclimation - they adjust their cellular nutrient:carbon ratios and photosynthetic machinery
based on light availability. Under low light, phytoplankton need higher nutrient concentrations to maintain growth because they must invest more in light-harvesting machinery. This creates an interactive effect between  
light and nutrient limitation that isn't captured by simple multiplication.                                                                                                                                                 

                                                                                                    Proposed Improvement                                                                                                    

Implement a light-dependent nutrient requirement where the half-saturation constant for nutrient uptake (K_N) increases under low light conditions. This represents the ecological reality that phytoplankton have higher   
nutrient demands when light-limited because they need to build more photosynthetic apparatus per unit growth.                                                                                                               

Mathematical form: K_N_effective = K_N * (1 + alpha_LN * (K_I / (avg_light + epsilon)))                                                                                                                                     

Where alpha_LN is a dimensionless coefficient representing the strength of light-nutrient interaction.                                                                                                                      

This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                                                                                                     

 1 It captures photoacclimation dynamics                                                                                                                                                                                    
 2 It explains why nutrient limitation is often more severe in deep or turbid waters                                                                                                                                        
 3 It creates a more realistic coupling between light and nutrient availability                                                                                                                                             
 4 It's mathematically simple (one additional parameter) but mechanistically meaningful
