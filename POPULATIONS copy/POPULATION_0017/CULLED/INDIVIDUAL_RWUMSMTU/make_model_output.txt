 • Holling Type II grazing by zooplankton

The parameter r (maximum phytoplankton growth rate) has been updated from literature with a value of 1.0 day⁻¹ and bounds [0.8, 1.2]. This is a relatively high growth rate, suggesting the phytoplankton community may be  
fast-growing species (e.g., small diatoms or flagellates).                                                                                                                                                                  

Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                                                                                                 

The current model lacks light limitation of phytoplankton growth. In oceanic mixed layers, phytoplankton growth is typically co-limited by both nutrients AND light availability. The current formulation only considers    
nutrient limitation, which is unrealistic for surface ocean dynamics where:                                                                                                                                                 

 1 Light varies with depth and season                                                                                                                                                                                       
 2 Self-shading occurs at high phytoplankton densities                                                                                                                                                                      
 3 Growth rates are modulated by the product of nutrient and light availability                                                                                                                                             

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add a self-shading light limitation term that creates a more realistic representation of phytoplankton growth. This will:                                                                                                   

 • Introduce a carrying capacity effect through light attenuation                                                                                                                                                           
 • Create more realistic bloom dynamics                                                                                                                                                                                     
 • Better represent the dual limitation (nutrients + light) characteristic of oceanic systems                                                                                                                               
 • Add a stabilizing mechanism that prevents unrealistic exponential growth                                                                                                                                                 

This is implemented using a simple exponential light attenuation model where phytoplankton biomass reduces light availability (Beer-Lambert law).

 +Added a light limitation factor: L = exp(-k_w * z - k_c * P * z)
