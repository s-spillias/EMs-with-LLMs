Looking at the current NPZ (Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton) model:                                                                                                                                                      

 1 Current Structure: The model includes nutrient limitation, light limitation with self-shading, temperature effects via Q10, grazing with Type II functional response, and multiple mortality pathways.                   
 2 Parameter Updates: I notice that parameter r (phytoplankton growth rate) has been updated from literature with a value of 1.0 day^-1 and bounds [0.8, 1.2]. The parameter m_Z (zooplankton quadratic mortality) has a    
   very specific value of 2.426 with tight bounds [2.276, 2.576], suggesting this came from calibration or literature.                                                                                                      
 3 Missing Process: A critical ecological process that's missing is nutrient remineralization from sloppy feeding. In real marine systems, zooplankton don't just excrete nutrients from unassimilated food (already        
   captured by the (1-e)*grazing term), but they also release dissolved organic matter during the feeding process itself through "sloppy feeding" - the mechanical breakage of phytoplankton cells during grazing. This can 
   account for 10-40% of nutrient regeneration in productive systems.                                                                                                                                                       
 4 Ecological Justification: Sloppy feeding is particularly important because:                                                                                                                                              
    • It creates a direct, immediate nutrient feedback during active grazing                                                                                                                                                
    • It's proportional to grazing intensity, not just inefficiency                                                                                                                                                         
    • It can sustain phytoplankton growth even under high grazing pressure                                                                                                                                                  
    • It's a well-documented process in marine ecology (Lampert 1978, Strom et al. 1997)                                                                                                                                    


                                                                                                    Proposed Improvement                                                                                                    

I will add a sloppy feeding parameter (β) that represents the fraction of grazed phytoplankton that is immediately released as dissolved nutrients during the feeding process, separate from the assimilation efficiency    
pathway.
