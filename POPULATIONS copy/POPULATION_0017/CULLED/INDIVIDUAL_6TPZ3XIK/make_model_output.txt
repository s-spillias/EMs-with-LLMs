                                                                                     Proposed Improvement: Light-Nutrient Co-limitation                                                                                     

I propose implementing a multiplicative co-limitation model where phytoplankton growth is limited by the minimum of light and nutrient availability. This is ecologically realistic because:                                

 1 Liebig's Law of the Minimum: Growth is limited by the scarcest resource                                                                                                                                                  
 2 Light attenuation: Light decreases exponentially with depth and is affected by phytoplankton self-shading                                                                                                                
 3 Seasonal dynamics: Captures the interplay between winter mixing (high nutrients, low light) and summer stratification (low nutrients, high light)                                                                        

The mathematical implementation will use:                                                                                                                                                                                   

 • Light availability as a function of surface irradiance and attenuation                                                                                                                                                   
 • Self-shading by phytoplankton (Beer-Lambert law)                                                                                                                                                                         
 • Multiplicative limitation: growth = r × f(N) × f(I)                                                                                                                                                                      

This adds ecological realism without excessive complexity and is well-supported in plankton ecology literature.
