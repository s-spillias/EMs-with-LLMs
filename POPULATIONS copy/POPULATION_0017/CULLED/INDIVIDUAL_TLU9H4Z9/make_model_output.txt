Current Model Structure:                                                                                                                                                                                                    

The model simulates a nutrient-phytoplankton-zooplankton (NPZ) system with:                                                                                                                                                 

 • Nutrient-limited phytoplankton growth with light co-limitation                                                                                                                                                           
 • Zooplankton grazing (Holling Type II)                                                                                                                                                                                    
 • Nutrient recycling from mortality                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Light attenuation with phytoplankton self-shading                                                                                                                                                                        

Key Observations:                                                                                                                                                                                                           

 1 Light Model: The current implementation uses Beer-Lambert law with self-shading, which is ecologically sound. However, the average light calculation in the mixed layer could be improved.                               
 2 Temperature Dependency: A critical missing component is temperature effects on biological rates. Ocean temperature strongly influences metabolic rates, growth, and grazing - this is a fundamental control in plankton  
   dynamics.                                                                                                                                                                                                                
 3 Parameter Values: Most parameters remain at initial estimates. The growth rate (r=1.0 day⁻¹), grazing rate (g_max=0.5 day⁻¹), and mortality rates appear reasonable for temperate waters.                                

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

I propose adding temperature-dependent rate modulation using Q10 formulation. This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                       

 • Temperature is a master variable controlling metabolic rates in ectotherms                                                                                                                                               
 • Q10 effects (rate doubling per 10°C) are well-documented for phytoplankton growth (Q10 ≈ 1.5-2.5) and zooplankton grazing (Q10 ≈ 2.0-3.0)                                                                                
 • This allows the model to capture seasonal dynamics and climate change effects                                                                                                                                            
 • It's a simple, well-validated approach that doesn't over-complicate the model                                                                                                                                            

This improvement maintains the current model structure while adding a critical environmental driver that affects all biological rate processes.
