 • Strengths: The model has key pieces for COTS outbreaks: temperature-modified vital rates (Gaussian f_temp), Allee effect (s_Allee), food saturation (f_food), exogenous larval immigration modulated by conditions, and

Chosen improvement                                                                                                                                                                                                              

 • Add a one-year maturation delay for consumption-derived recruitment. Instead of adding eC*(gF+gS) directly to adults in year t, store it in a “recruitment buffer” this year and add a fraction tau_mature of last year’s    
   buffer to adults next year. This simple, biologically grounded delay can sharpen outbreak pulses and better match observed boom-bust timing without overcomplicating the model.
