 1 Do any code changes seem needed?                                                                                                                                                                                             

Yes. The model currently uses a constant light availability (L_avail), which is often too simplistic for mixed layers with appreciable phytoplankton. I propose adding phytoplankton self-shading so that effective light       
decreases with phytoplankton biomass. This is a well-supported environmental modifier that improves nutrient-light co-limitation realism in the mixed layer. This requires a new parameter k_shade and a change to how fL is    
computed.                                                                                                                                                                                                                       

 2 What changes are needed and why?                                                                                                                                                                                             

 • Assessment of fit: Without the residuals file I cannot quantify fit, but structurally this N-P-Z model is solid and should capture first-order behavior. Common issues when L_avail is constant include:                     
    • Overestimation of phytoplankton growth during blooms (no self-shading feedback), leading to too-high peaks.                                                                                                               
    • Phase and amplitude mismatches in P and Z if light-driven bloom termination is not captured.                                                                                                                              
 • Project context coverage: The model includes nutrient uptake with saturation, temperature scaling via Q10, zooplankton functional response (Type II/III via Hill exponent), mortality, excretion, mixed-layer exchange, and  
   some fast recycling pathways. This addresses many key processes for the mixed layer.                                                                                                                                         
 • Missing/oversimplified processes:                                                                                                                                                                                            
    • Self-shading of light by phytoplankton (currently missing).                                                                                                                                                               
    • No detrital/DOM pools and sinking; all recycling is instantaneous (may bias nutrient regeneration timing).                                                                                                                
    • A single Q10 for multiple processes; literature often supports different temperature sensitivities for growth vs. grazing vs. mortality.                                                                                  
    • Constant assimilation efficiency (AE_Z) rather than food/temperature-dependent.                                                                                                                                           
    • Quadratic mortality not temperature-modified (could be intentional if representing predation by higher trophic levels).                                                                                                   
 • Updated literature values and structure: Q10 was updated from literature, but a single Q10 applied to all (linear) biological rates may not be ideal ecologically. For now, I am not splitting Q10 to maintain parsimony; I  
   note it as a future enhancement if residuals suggest process-specific temperature biases.                                                                                                                                    

Chosen improvement: Add phytoplankton self-shading of light.                                                                                                                                                                    

 • Ecological rationale: As phytoplankton biomass increases, light is attenuated in the mixed layer, reducing further phytoplankton growth. This negative feedback moderates bloom amplitude and can improve timing and         
   magnitude of P and Z trajectories.                                                                                                                                                                                           
 • Mathematical change: Replace the constant light term with an effective light:                                                                                                                                                
   L_eff = L_avail * exp(-k_shade * P_prev)                                                                                                                                                                                     
   Then compute the light limitation term as:                                                                                                                                                                                   
   fL = L_eff / (K_L + L_eff + tiny)                                                                                                                                                                                            
   This preserves the original saturating form and co-limitation structure, but introduces a realistic dependence on biomass.                                                                                                   
 • New parameter:                                                                                                                                                                                                               
   k_shade (m^3 (g C)^-1): light attenuation per unit phytoplankton biomass. Initial estimate 0.5; bounds [0, 5]. Adds a mild prior-like smooth penalty within bounds.
