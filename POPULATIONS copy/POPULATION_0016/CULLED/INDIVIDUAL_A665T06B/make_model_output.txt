1. Model Assessment                                                                                                                                                                                                         

The current model is a standard Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton (NPZ) model. It captures the core dynamics of a simple plankton food web using established formulations like Michaelis-Menten for nutrient uptake and    
Holling Type II for grazing. The inclusion of quadratic mortality for zooplankton is a good feature, representing density-dependent losses.                                                                                 

However, a key simplification is the phytoplankton mortality term, which is purely linear (mu_P * P). This overlooks important density-dependent processes that often regulate phytoplankton blooms in nature, such as viral
lysis or mass sinking due to aggregation at high cell densities. This simplification can lead to unstable model behavior and unrealistic, unbounded phytoplankton growth.                                                   

2. Proposed Ecological Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                          

I will introduce a quadratic mortality term for phytoplankton. This adds a density-dependent self-regulation mechanism to the phytoplankton population, making the model more stable and ecologically realistic.            

This change involves:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

 • Adding a new parameter, mu_P_quad, to represent the rate of quadratic mortality.                                                                                                                                         
 • Updating the phytoplankton differential equation (dP/dt) to include this new loss term: - mu_P_quad * P^2.                                                                                                               
 • Updating the nutrient differential equation (dN/dt) to include the remineralization from this new mortality term (+ mu_P_quad * P^2), maintaining the model's mass balance, consistent with how other mortality terms are
   handled.                                                                                                                                                                                                                 

These changes are documented in intention.txt, parameters.json, and model.cpp below.
