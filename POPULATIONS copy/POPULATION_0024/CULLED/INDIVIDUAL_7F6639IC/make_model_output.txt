   real COTS outbreaks can occur when conditions are favorable but not necessarily optimal across all dimensions.                                                                                                           
 2 Missing Larval Pool Dynamics: The model treats recruitment as instantaneous, but COTS have a planktonic larval stage lasting weeks. A larval pool that accumulates over time could better explain delayed outbreak       
   responses and the characteristic lag between favorable conditions and population explosions.

   literature-updated bounds. However, the temperature effect uses a simple Gaussian that may not capture the asymmetric thermal tolerance of larvae (sharp decline above optimum, gradual decline below).                  

Key Ecological Process Missing:

 • Accumulation of competent larvae during favorable periods                                                                                                                                                                
 • Delayed settlement when substrate and food conditions align                                                                                                                                                              
 • The ability for larvae to "wait" for optimal settlement cues                                                                                                                                                             

This creates the temporal decoupling between spawning conditions and recruitment that drives the episodic nature of outbreaks.
