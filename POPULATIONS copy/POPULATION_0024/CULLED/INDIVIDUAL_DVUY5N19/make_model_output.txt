                                                                               Proposed Improvement: Immigration Rescue from Allee Threshold                                                                                

Ecological Reasoning:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Crown-of-Thorns outbreaks on the Great Barrier Reef are often initiated by larval connectivity between reefs. A critical but missing mechanism is the "rescue effect" where high larval immigration can overcome local Allee
thresholds and trigger outbreaks even when resident populations are low. This is particularly important for episodic outbreak dynamics because:                                                                             

 1 Spatial coupling drives outbreaks - Reefs downstream of outbreak sources receive larval pulses                                                                                                                           
 2 Immigration bypasses Allee constraints - External larvae don't require local mating success                                                                                                                              
 3 Threshold crossing - Immigration can push populations above critical densities needed for self-sustaining growth                                                                                                         

Mathematical Implementation:                                                                                                                                                                                                

I'll modify the Allee effect to include an immigration rescue term that allows the effective density to include both resident and immigrant contributions, creating a pathway for outbreak initiation even when local       
density is below threshold.
