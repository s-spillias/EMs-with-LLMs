                                                                          Key Ecological Improvement: Non-linear Resource-Recruitment Relationship                                                                          

The current model uses a linear coral facilitation term that doesn't capture the ecological reality of juvenile COTS survival. Research shows:                                                                              

 • Juvenile COTS need a minimum coral threshold to survive settlement                                                                                                                                                       
 • Above this threshold, recruitment success increases rapidly                                                                                                                                                              
 • At high coral cover, recruitment saturates (space/shelter limitations)

 3 Saturating at high coral cover

 +- Near-zero recruitment below the coral threshold                                                                                                                                                                         
 +- Rapid increase in recruitment success above threshold                                                                                                                                                                   
 +- Saturation at high coral cover
