                                                                                                 Current Model Performance                                                                                                  

Looking at the model structure, I can identify several key features:                                                                                                                                                        

 1 Outbreak dynamics: The model includes threshold-based recruitment amplification (Hill function) that should create episodic outbreaks                                                                                    
 2 Coral predation: Type II functional responses with prey switching between fast and slow coral                                                                                                                            
 3 Environmental drivers: Temperature effects on both COTS recruitment and coral stress                                                                                                                                     
 4 Immigration: External larval supply weighted against local reproduction                                                                                                                                                  

                                                                                                   Key Issues Identified                                                                                                    

 1 Parameter value inconsistency: The log_temp_opt parameter has been updated from literature (marked as updated_from_literature: true) to 3.3495 (≈28.4°C). However, the current Gaussian temperature effect (Equation 2)  
   may not adequately capture the asymmetric thermal response of COTS larvae - they typically have sharp upper thermal limits but broader tolerance below the optimum.                                                      
 2 Missing density-dependent predation efficiency: The current model assumes constant attack rates and handling times regardless of COTS density. During outbreaks, high COTS densities can lead to interference competition
   and reduced per-capita feeding efficiency - a critical mechanism for outbreak collapse.                                                                                                                                  
 3 Oversimplified starvation dynamics: Equation 14 uses a simple exponential starvation factor, but this doesn't capture the lag between coral depletion and COTS mortality, nor does it account for COTS ability to survive
   extended periods without food.                                                                                                                                                                                           

                                                                               Proposed Improvement: Density-Dependent Predation Interference                                                                               

The most critical missing mechanism for capturing boom-bust dynamics is predation interference at high COTS densities. This is ecologically well-documented: when COTS are crowded during outbreaks, they interfere with    
each other's feeding, reducing per-capita consumption rates. This creates a negative feedback that contributes to outbreak collapse even before coral is fully depleted.                                                    

I'll implement this through:                                                                                                                                                                                                

 1 A new parameter interference_strength that scales the reduction in attack rates with COTS density                                                                                                                        
 2 Modification of attack rates to decline with COTS density using a rational function                                                                                                                                      
 3 This will make outbreaks self-limiting through behavioral interference, not just resource depletion                                                                                                                      

This improvement is mathematically simple (one new parameter, minor equation modification) but ecologically powerful for capturing the characteristic rapid collapse of COTS outbreaks.
