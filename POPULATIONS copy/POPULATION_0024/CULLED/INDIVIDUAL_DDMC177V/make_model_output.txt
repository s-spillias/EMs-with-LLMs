                                                                                                         Assessment                                                                                                         

                                                                                                 Current Model Performance                                                                                                  

The model implements a sophisticated COTS-coral predator-prey system with:                                                                                                                                                  

 • Allee effects for COTS at low densities                                                                                                                                                                                  
 • Temperature-dependent recruitment                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Threshold-based outbreak dynamics (Hill function)                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Type II functional responses for predation                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Resource-dependent recruitment facilitation                                                                                                                                                                              

                                                                                                   Key Issues Identified                                                                                                    

 1 Temperature effect on COTS is overly optimistic: The log_temp_opt parameter was updated from literature to 3.3495 (≈28.4°C), but the current Gaussian temperature response (Equation 2) allows COTS recruitment at a wide
   range of temperatures. This doesn't capture the critical observation that COTS outbreaks are strongly associated with warm water events and nutrient pulses.                                                             
 2 Missing nutrient-temperature interaction: Literature on COTS outbreaks emphasizes that warm temperatures alone aren't sufficient - they need to coincide with nutrient availability (often from flood plumes) to trigger 
   the phytoplankton blooms that feed COTS larvae. The current model treats temperature as a simple Gaussian optimum curve.                                                                                                 
 3 Outbreak initiation mechanism may be too sensitive: The Hill function (Equation 5) with recruitment_cooperativity = 3.0 creates very sharp transitions, but the immigration_weight = 2.0 means immigration is weighted   
   twice as heavily as local reproduction. This could trigger outbreaks too easily from immigration alone.                                                                                                                  

                                                                             Proposed Improvement: Temperature-Dependent Larval Survival Window                                                                             

Instead of a simple Gaussian temperature response, I'll implement a temperature window mechanism where:                                                                                                                     

 • Below optimal temperature: recruitment is severely limited (cold water reduces larval development and survival)                                                                                                          
 • At optimal temperature: recruitment is maximized                                                                                                                                                                         
 • Above optimal temperature: recruitment remains high (warm water extends larval survival and development)                                                                                                                 
 • Only at extreme temperatures: recruitment declines (bleaching-level temperatures)                                                                                                                                        

This captures the ecological reality that COTS outbreaks are associated with warm water events that extend larval survival periods and increase food availability, rather than a narrow optimal temperature range.          

I'll modify the temperature effect to use an asymmetric response function that better represents the biology:                                                                                                               

 • Sharp decline below optimal temperature (cold-sensitive larvae)                                                                                                                                                          
 • Plateau at and above optimal temperature (warm water benefits)                                                                                                                                                           
 • Only decline at extreme high temperatures
