                                                                                                 Current Model Performance                                                                                                  

The model captures COTS-coral dynamics with:                                                                                                                                                                                

 • Allee effects for low-density COTS populations                                                                                                                                                                           
 • Temperature-dependent recruitment                                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Immigration enhancement

 • A recruitment pulse mechanism with composite favorability index                                                                                                                                                          

                                                                                                   Key Issues Identified                                                                                                    

 1 Recruitment Pulse Mechanism: The current implementation (Equations 11-18) uses a multiplicative favorability index where all three factors (temperature, immigration, food) must be simultaneously high. This is overly  
   restrictive and may miss outbreak initiation scenarios where one or two factors are dominant.                                                                                                                            
 2 Parameter Updates: The log_mort_base parameter has been updated from literature with tight bounds (-1.204 to -0.511, corresponding to 0.3-0.6 year⁻¹), and log_temp_opt has been refined to 3.3495 (28.5°C). These

 3 Missing Ecological Process: The model lacks density-dependent recruitment success beyond the Allee effect. In real COTS outbreaks, intermediate densities can facilitate recruitment through aggregation benefits        
   (fertilization success, predator swamping) before density-dependent mortality dominates at high densities. This creates a "sweet spot" for outbreak amplification.                                                       

                                                                              Proposed Improvement: Dome-Shaped Density-Dependent Recruitment                                                                               

I'll add a dome-shaped recruitment facilitation function that captures the non-linear relationship between adult COTS density and recruitment success. This addresses the PROJECT CONTEXT emphasis on "mechanisms that      
trigger and sustain outbreak events" by:                                                                                                                                                                                    

 1 Allowing recruitment pulses to be more effective at intermediate densities (outbreak amplification)                                                                                                                      
 2 Reducing recruitment at very high densities (negative feedback preventing unrealistic explosions)                                                                                                                        
 3 Maintaining Allee effects at low densities                                                                                                                                                                               
 4 Creating more realistic boom-bust dynamics                                                                                                                                                                               

This is ecologically justified by:                                                                                                                                                                                          

 • Aggregation benefits for fertilization at intermediate densities                                                                                                                                                         
 • Predator swamping effects during outbreak initiation                                                                                                                                                                     
 • Resource depletion and larval competition at extreme densities
