    • Peaks under- or over-predicted (timing and magnitude),                                                                                                                                                                    
    • Crashes that are too slow (fat tails after peaks),                                                                                                                                                                        
    • Coral composition misfit during/after outbreaks (too much or too little Acropora loss).

 2 Alignment with the project context                                                                                                                                                                                           

 • The model already includes multiple mechanisms central to boom-bust dynamics:                                                                                                                                                
    • Food- and temperature-modulated recruitment with an Allee effect,                                                                                                                                                         
    • Space-limited coral growth, selective predation, and bleaching mortality,                                                                                                                                                 
    • Carrying capacity linked to coral cover and external larval immigration.                                                                                                                                                  
 • This is largely appropriate for episodic COTS outbreaks. However, the decline phase (bust) may be overly governed by reduced growth (via Kc) and baseline mortality, potentially making crashes too gradual when prey is     
   depleted.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    

 3 Potentially missing or oversimplified processes                                                                                                                                                                              

 • Starvation/disease-driven mortality during food scarcity: Outbreaks are often followed by rapid declines due to prey depletion, starvation, and disease. The current model reduces growth via carrying capacity as coral     
   declines but does not increase mortality when intake per predator becomes low.                                                                                                                                               
 • Recruitment delay structure: Outbreaks often reflect multi-year developmental lags. The current model uses a 1-year mixture lag; a distributed lag kernel could be explored later if needed.                                 
 • Minor consistency issue: Topt penalty bounds in model.cpp were broader than the updated literature bounds in parameters.json.                                                                                                

Chosen single ecological improvement                                                                                                                                                                                            

Add food-limitation-driven mortality for COTS that increases when per-predator intake is low. This directly targets fast post-peak declines without requiring new data. It preserves existing outbreak triggers (food and       
temperature) while improving the bust dynamics.                                                                                                                                                                                 

Mathematical form                                                                                                                                                                                                               

 • Compute the same per-predator intake saturation index as already used for reproduction: I_sat in [0, 1].                                                                                                                     
 • Add an extra mortality component that increases as food availability drops:                                                                                                                                                  
   mC_extra = mC_starve_max * (max(eps, 1 - I_sat))^theta_starve                                                                                                                                                                
 • Effective COTS mortality:                                                                                                                                                                                                    
   mC_eff = mC_base + mC_extra                                                                                                                                                                                                  
 • Update equation:                                                                                                                                                                                                             
   C_next_raw = C_prev + deltaC - mC_eff * C_prev + gamma_imm * IMM_t                                                                                                                                                           
 • This is AD-safe, uses only predicted states and drivers, and avoids data leakage.                                                                                                                                            

Parameter updates                                                                                                                                                                                                               

 • New parameters:                                                                                                                                                                                                              
    • mC_starve_max (year^-1): maximum additional starvation/disease mortality at zero intake,                                                                                                                                  
    • theta_starve (dimensionless): curvature controlling sensitivity to food shortage.                                                                                                                                         
 • Also aligned Topt bound penalties in model.cpp with parameters.json updated literature bounds (28–30 °C).                                                                                                                    

Why this improves ecological realism                                                                                                                                                                                            

 • Outbreak busts are sharper when prey is depleted due to starvation and disease; scaling mortality with food scarcity captures this feedback explicitly, improving timing and duration of the decline phase while maintaining 
   mechanistic parsimony.
