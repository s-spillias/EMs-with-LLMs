    • Mis-time or over-persist outbreaks because per-capita feeding remains high even at very high COTS densities.

 • Gaps for boom-bust realism:                                                                                                                                                                                                  
    • No mutual interference among COTS during outbreaks. Field studies indicate interference (e.g., aggregation, obstruction, competition for space/handling) can reduce per-capita feeding at high predator density. This     
      typically shapes the peak, duration, and decline phase of outbreaks—central to boom-bust dynamics.                                                                                                                        
    • Bleaching impacts only reduce intrinsic coral growth, not direct coral mortality, which can understate rapid coral loss under extreme heat events. This is secondary to boom-bust timing but relevant for amplitude.      

 3 Potentially missing or oversimplified processes                                                                                                                                                                              

 • Missing mutual interference in predation (Holling II lacks a predator-density term in the denominator).                                                                                                                      
 • No explicit juvenile stage for COTS; maturation is represented via a 1-year lag buffer which is acceptable for parsimony.                                                                                                    
 • Bleaching as a growth reduction only (no direct mortality term). Could be added later if residuals show underestimation of coral losses during hot years.                                                                    

Chosen improvement and rationale                                                                                                                                                                                                

 • Approach: Higher-order representation of predation via a Beddington–DeAngelis (BD) functional response by adding a predator-density interference term to the denominator of the consumption function.                        
 • Why: BD is a minimal, ecologically grounded extension that reduces per-capita consumption at high COTS densities. This can:                                                                                                  
    • Temper unrealistically high peaks in coral loss during outbreaks.                                                                                                                                                         
    • Shorten or delay the over-persistence of peak consumption.                                                                                                                                                                
    • Better match observed boom-bust timing/magnitude without complicating state structure.                                                                                                                                    
 • Mathematical change: Replace Holling II denominator:                                                                                                                                                                         
    • From: 1 + h(a_f F + a_s S)                                                                                                                                                                                                
    • To:   1 + h(a_f F + a_s S) + i_interf * C                                                                                                                                                                                 
   where C is adult COTS density and i_interf is an interference strength parameter (new, constrained ≥ 0).                                                                                                                     
 • This retains existing temperature and food effects on recruitment and preserves the no-data-leakage property (all drivers use t-1 values).
