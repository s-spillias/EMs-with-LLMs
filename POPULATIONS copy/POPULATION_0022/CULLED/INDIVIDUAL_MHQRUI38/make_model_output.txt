 +**Model Assessment:**                                                                                                                                                                                                     
 +The current model provides a solid foundation for simulating Crown-of-Thorns Starfish (COTS) and coral dynamics. It includes key processes such as density-dependent COTS mortality (starvation), selective predation on  
 different coral types, and logistic growth of corals influenced by sea-surface temperature.                                                                                                                                
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +However, the model has a significant structural limitation regarding the project's goal of capturing episodic outbreaks. The COTS population dynamics rely exclusively on an external forcing variable, `cotsimm_dat`     
 (larval immigration), for any population increase. This means the model cannot generate an outbreak endogenously. It can only simulate an outbreak if a large pulse of immigrants is provided in the data. This approach   
 misses the critical positive feedback loop where a large, established COTS population on a reef produces a massive number of larvae, leading to a local recruitment event that sustains and amplifies the outbreak. This   
 self-sustaining "boom" phase is a hallmark of COTS population ecology.                                                                                                                                                     
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +**Proposed Ecological Improvement:**                                                                                                                                                                                      
 +To address this, I will introduce a local reproduction and recruitment mechanism into the COTS population dynamics. This change will allow the COTS population to grow based on its own density and the availability of   
 resources, creating a more realistic feedback loop for outbreak generation.                                                                                                                                                
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +**Justification and Implementation:**                                                                                                                                                                                     
 +The improvement will be implemented by adding a new term to the COTS state equation that represents local recruitment. This term will be calculated as:                                                                   
 +`local_recruitment = cots_repro_rate * cots_pred(t-1) * (total_coral_prev / K_coral_total)`                                                                                                                               
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +- `cots_repro_rate` is a new parameter representing the intrinsic per-capita reproductive rate of COTS.                                                                                                                   
 +- `cots_pred(t-1)` is the adult COTS population from the previous year, which constitutes the spawning stock.                                                                                                             
 +- `(total_coral_prev / K_coral_total)` is the relative coral cover, which serves as a proxy for two critical factors for successful recruitment: 1) availability of settlement habitat (juveniles often settle on crustos 
 coralline algae associated with healthy reefs) and 2) food availability for newly settled juveniles.                                                                                                                       
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +This formulation introduces a crucial positive feedback: more adult COTS lead to more recruits, driving population growth. It also includes a density-dependent check: as corals are consumed (`total_coral_prev`         
 decreases), recruitment success declines, helping to create the "bust" part of the cycle along with the existing starvation mechanism. This single modification allows the model to better simulate the characteristic     
 boom-bust dynamics of COTS outbreaks from first principles, rather than being entirely dependent on an external driver.                                                                                                    
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +A new parameter, `cots_repro_rate`, will be added to `parameters.json` as an initial estimate to be fitted, allowing the model to determine the strength of this reproductive feedback from the data.
