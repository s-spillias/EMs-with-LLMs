                                                                                               Assessment and Proposed Change                                                                                               

1. Model Assessment                                                                                                                                                                                                         

The current model establishes a robust predator-prey framework between Crown-of-Thorns Starfish (COTS) and two coral groups. It correctly incorporates essential dynamics such as COTS starvation, larval immigration as an 
external driver, and selective predation.                                                                                                                                                                                   

However, a key area for improvement lies in the COTS local recruitment dynamics. The current Beverton-Holt formulation assumes that the highest per-capita reproductive success occurs at the lowest population densities.  
For a broadcast spawner like COTS, this is biologically unrealistic. At very low densities, fertilization success is limited by the distance between individuals, a phenomenon known as an Allee effect. This simplification
limits the model's ability to capture the characteristic boom-bust cycle, specifically the mechanism that keeps the population at low levels for extended periods and the sharp "trigger" that initiates an outbreak.       

2. Proposed Ecological Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                          

I will introduce a component Allee effect into the COTS local recruitment function. This change will model the reduced reproductive success at low population densities, providing a more realistic trigger mechanism for   
population outbreaks.                                                                                                                                                                                                       

3. Rationale and Implementation                                                                                                                                                                                             

An Allee effect introduces a critical density threshold. Below this threshold, the population's reproductive rate is too low to overcome mortality, keeping numbers suppressed. An external event, such as a larval         
immigration pulse (already modeled via cotsimm_dat), can push the density above this threshold, allowing for rapid population growth and triggering an outbreak. This directly addresses the project's goal of modeling the 
mechanisms that initiate episodic outbreaks.                                                                                                                                                                                

The implementation will involve:                                                                                                                                                                                            

 1 Adding a new parameter cots_allee to parameters.json to define the density at which the Allee effect is most pronounced.                                                                                                 
 2 Updating model.cpp to include this parameter and modify the COTS recruitment equation. The existing Beverton-Holt term will be multiplied by a sigmoid function that reduces recruitment at low COTS densities.          
 3 Updating intention.txt to document this assessment and rationale.
