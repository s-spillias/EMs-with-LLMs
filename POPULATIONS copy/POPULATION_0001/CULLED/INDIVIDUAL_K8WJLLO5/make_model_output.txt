 starvation mortality, immigration pulses with lags, and a multi-prey functional response). These can produce realistic outbreak shapes in principle, but without

 +                                                                                                                                                                      
 +2) Alignment with project context                                                                                                                                     
 +- Outbreak triggers: The model uses (i) an exogenous immigration pulse with a short lag kernel, (ii) fecundity boosted by per-capita feeding, and (iii) a hump-shaped 
 SST effect on COTS net growth. This helps initiate booms under favorable conditions.                                                                                   
 +- Bust dynamics: The model includes coral depletion feedbacks (lower K_A when coral is low), high-density extra mortality, and starvation mortality at low            
 intake—mechanisms that can drive rapid declines.                                                                                                                       
 +- Coral selectivity and community response: The two-guild coral structure (fast Acropora vs slow Porites/Faviidae) with a multi-prey functional response and distinct 
 predation conversion factors captures selective feeding and differential decline.                                                                                      
 +- Missing linkage: A key ecological pathway not yet represented is the dependence of post-settlement survival (settlement success/early juvenile survival) on habitat 
 quality (live coral structure). Literature and field observations indicate that outbreak initiation and persistence rely on habitat conditions, not just larval supply 
 This coupling helps explain site-level differences in outbreak amplification and subsequent collapse when coral structure is lost.                                     
 +                                                                                                                                                                      
 +3) Potential missing or oversimplified processes                                                                                                                      
 +- Habitat-coupled settlement/early survival: Immigration currently adds adults with a time-lag kernel and constant conversion gamma_I, independent of habitat. This   
 likely over-simplifies the trigger/sustain phases by ignoring that post-settlement survival is higher when structurally complex coral (especially Acropora) is availab 
 and lower after coral loss.                                                                                                                                            
 +- Temperature sensitivity of foraging: Attack rates could modestly increase with temperature within normal ranges; however, adding this now would introduce additiona 
 parameters without strong constraints from the provided data streams.

 +                                                                                                                                                                      
 +Chosen improvement                                                                                                                                                    
 +                                                                                                                                                                      
 +Approach: Environmental modifier of process (habitat-dependent settlement/early survival multiplier on immigration)                                                   
 +- Rationale: Outbreak initiation often requires both larval supply and favorable habitat for settlement/early survival. As coral (especially fast-growing Acropora)   
 declines, the survival multiplier should fall, damping new recruitment and hastening the bust. Conversely, high coral cover should amplify the effect of immigration   
 pulses and support outbreak growth.                                                                                                                                    
 +- Mathematical form: Multiply the immigration term by a saturating function of weighted coral cover at the previous time step:                                        
 +  Let H_{t-1} = wK_F * F_{t-1} + wK_S * S_{t-1}  (weighted % coral cover).                                                                                            
 +  settle_mult(H) = eps_settle + (1 - eps_settle) * H / (H + C_half_settle)                                                                                            
 +  where eps_settle ∈ [0,1] is the minimal fraction of immigration that succeeds when coral is nearly absent, and C_half_settle is the half-saturation of habitat (%   
 cover). This form:                                                                                                                                                     
 +  - avoids data leakage (uses only t-1 states),                                                                                                                       
 +  - is bounded and smooth (identifiable in TMB),                                                                                                                      
 +  - increases adult additions when suitable habitat is present, and                                                                                                   
 +  - reduces additions as coral structure collapses during busts.                                                                                                      
 +- Implementation details:                                                                                                                                             
 +  - Compute H_{t-1} from the model-predicted F_{t-1}, S_{t-1} (weighted by existing wK_F, wK_S).                                                                      
 +  - Compute settle_mult with soft clipping for numerical stability.                                                                                                   
 +  - Multiply the existing lagged immigration term by settle_mult before adding to adults.                                                                             
 +                                                                                                                                                                      
 +Expected qualitative effect                                                                                                                                           
 +- Outbreak timing and magnitude: Immigration pulses lead to stronger adult increases when coral cover is high; this helps capture rapid onset when habitat is         
 favorable.                                                                                                                                                             
 +- Bust duration and depth: After a severe coral decline, immigration contributes less to adult numbers, reinforcing the collapse and yielding more realistic bust     
 tails.                                                                                                                                                                 
 +- Selective predation feedback: Because H_{t-1} includes the guild weights already informing K_A, fast coral (Acropora) loss has a proportionally larger dampening    
 effect on settlement, aligning with observed patterns.                                                                                                                 
 +
