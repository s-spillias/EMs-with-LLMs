I have reviewed the model and propose one targeted ecological improvement to better represent outbreak dynamics while avoiding data leakage.                            

Assessment summary:                                                                                                                                                     

 • Fit to data: I don’t have the residuals file in this chat, so I can’t quantify fit. Please provide it and I will refine this assessment. Conceptually, the model can 
   generate boom-bust cycles via consumption-driven fecundity, Allee effects, carrying capacity tied to coral, and starvation mortality. However, a linear fecundity    
   boost from per-capita consumption can cause unrealistically high reproduction when coral is abundant, potentially leading to overshoot and prolonged outbreaks.      
 • Alignment with project context: The model already addresses key mechanisms for outbreaks (density dependence via carrying capacity and Allee effect, feeding-driven  
   recruitment, immigration pulses with a lag, temperature modifiers, and coral guild-specific predation). It is structurally capable of timing and magnitude control,  
   but the linear fecundity boost likely limits realism for peak magnitude and decay rate.                                                                              
 • Missing/oversimplified processes:                                                                                                                                    
    • Larval survival pulses linked to nutrient/runoff events (not explicitly modeled; partially captured by cotsimm_dat).                                              
    • Maturation/cryptic juvenile stage beyond one year delay (only a 1-year lag is included for immigration).                                                          
    • Disease/density-dependent mortality at high A (partly represented by starvation; disease not explicit).                                                           
    • Fecundity saturation vs food availability (currently linear; lacks diminishing returns at high per-capita feeding).                                               

Change chosen: Introduce a saturating fecundity boost (Michaelis-Menten form) that limits maximum fecundity enhancement at high feeding while retaining sensitivity when
coral food is scarce. This is a resource-limitation mechanism that is ecologically justified (egg production and successful spawning scale sublinearly with energetic   
intake and often exhibit diminishing returns).
