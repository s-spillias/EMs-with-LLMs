Yes. To better capture outbreak magnitude and duration without data leakage, I will add predator interference to the COTS consumption process using a Beddington–DeAngelis style denominator. This introduces a single new parameter controlling the    
strength of intraspecific interference among COTS, which is ecologically plausible and helps prevent unrealistically high per-area consumption at peak densities.                                                                                       

 2 Explanation of changes                                                                                                                                                                                                                               

 • Rationale: The current Type III functional response already gives low consumption when edible coral is scarce, but at very high COTS densities the model can still produce unrealistically high total consumption, potentially shortening or         
   mis-timing bust phases. Observationally, dense COTS aggregations exhibit interference and reduced per-capita feeding rates due to crowding and overlapping foraging. Adding an interference term directly addresses this and improves the model’s    
   ability to produce realistic boom-bust cycles without changing the observation model or leaking information.                                                                                                                                         
 • Mathematical change: Replace total consumption cons_all = cons_per_pred * N_prev with a Beddington–DeAngelis style term: cons_all = (cons_base * N_prev) / (1 + iN * N_prev), where iN = exp(log_iN) ≥ 0 is the interference coefficient. This       
   reduces total consumption at high predator densities while preserving current behavior at low densities.                                                                                                                                             
 • Code changes:                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        
    • Add new parameter log_iN and its transform iN.                                                                                                                                                                                                    
    • Apply the interference denominator in both the initial diagnostics (t=0) and during the main time loop to maintain consistency.
