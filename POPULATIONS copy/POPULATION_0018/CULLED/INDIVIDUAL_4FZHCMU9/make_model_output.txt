The surface light intensity I_0 has been updated from literature to 600.0 μmol photons m^-2 s^-1 (with bounds 200-1000), suggesting this is a well-constrained parameter. However, the current model uses a simple          
Michaelis-Menten light limitation that doesn't account for photoinhibition - the well-documented phenomenon where excessive light can inhibit photosynthesis.                                                               

Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                                                                                                 

At high light intensities (which the updated I_0 value suggests are relevant), phytoplankton can experience photoinhibition due to photodamage of photosystem II. This is particularly important in shallow mixed layers or 
during high irradiance conditions. The current monotonic increasing light response (Michaelis-Menten) cannot capture this ecologically important process.                                                                   

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Replace the simple Michaelis-Menten light limitation with a Steele (1962) photosynthesis-irradiance curve that includes photoinhibition. This is a well-established formulation in biological oceanography:                 

f_light = (I_avg / I_opt) * exp(1 - I_avg / I_opt)                                                                                                                                                                          

where I_opt is the optimal light intensity for photosynthesis. This creates a peaked response where photosynthesis increases with light up to I_opt, then decreases at higher intensities due to photoinhibition.           

Ecological Justification:                                                                                                                                                                                                   

 1 More realistic representation of phytoplankton photophysiology                                                                                                                                                           
 2 Captures the trade-off between light limitation and photodamage                                                                                                                                                          
 3 Important for surface waters where I_0 values are high (as indicated by the updated parameter)                                                                                                                           
 4 Can explain phytoplankton vertical distribution patterns                                                                                                                                                                 
 5 Affects bloom dynamics and nutrient uptake patterns
