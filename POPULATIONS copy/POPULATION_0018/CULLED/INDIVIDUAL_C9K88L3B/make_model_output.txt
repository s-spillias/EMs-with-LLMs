                                                                                                Assessment of Current Model                                                                                                 

Current Structure:                                                                                                                                                                                                          

The model implements a classic NPZ (Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton) model with:                                                                                                                                         

 • Michaelis-Menten nutrient uptake by phytoplankton                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Holling Type II functional response for zooplankton grazing                                                                                                                                                              
 • Linear and quadratic zooplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Nutrient recycling from both phytoplankton and zooplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                     

Key Observation:                                                                                                                                                                                                            

Looking at the parameter values, I notice that the model currently treats phytoplankton growth as purely nutrient-limited. However, in oceanic mixed layers, light availability is often a co-limiting factor alongside     
nutrients. The current r_max parameter (0.5 day^-1) represents maximum growth under optimal conditions, but the model doesn't account for light attenuation through the water column or self-shading by phytoplankton       
biomass.                                                                                                                                                                                                                    

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add light limitation with self-shading to the phytoplankton growth term. This is ecologically critical because:                                                                                                             

 1 In the oceanic mixed layer, light decreases with depth                                                                                                                                                                   
 2 High phytoplankton concentrations create self-shading, limiting their own growth                                                                                                                                         
 3 This creates a realistic negative feedback mechanism that can stabilize blooms                                                                                                                                           
 4 It better represents the dual limitation (nutrients + light) that controls primary production                                                                                                                            

This improvement uses a multiplicative limitation approach where both nutrient AND light must be available for growth, which is more realistic than the current nutrient-only limitation.
