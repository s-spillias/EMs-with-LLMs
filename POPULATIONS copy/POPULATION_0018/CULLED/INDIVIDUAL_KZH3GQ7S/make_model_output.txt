                                                                                                Assessment of Current Model                                                                                                 

Current Structure:                                                                                                                                                                                                          

The model implements a classic NPZ (Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton) model with:                                                                                                                                         

 • Michaelis-Menten nutrient uptake by phytoplankton                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Holling Type II functional response for grazing                                                                                                                                                                          
 • Linear and quadratic zooplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Nutrient recycling from both trophic levels                                                                                                                                                                              

Key Observation:                                                                                                                                                                                                            

Looking at the parameter values, I notice that several parameters have been set with specific values from literature, but the model lacks a critical ecological process: light limitation of phytoplankton growth. In       
oceanic mixed layers, phytoplankton growth is typically co-limited by both nutrients AND light availability. The current model only considers nutrient limitation.                                                          

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

I will add light limitation to phytoplankton growth using a multiplicative co-limitation framework. This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                 

 1 In the oceanic mixed layer, light availability varies with depth and season                                                                                                                                              
 2 Phytoplankton growth is typically limited by the minimum of light or nutrients (Liebig's Law) or their multiplicative interaction                                                                                        
 3 This is a well-established process in marine ecosystem models (e.g., NPZD models)                                                                                                                                        

The improvement will use a simple light attenuation model where light decreases exponentially with depth and is attenuated by phytoplankton biomass (self-shading). I'll implement this as a multiplicative term that       
modifies the growth rate, representing the average light availability in the mixed layer.
