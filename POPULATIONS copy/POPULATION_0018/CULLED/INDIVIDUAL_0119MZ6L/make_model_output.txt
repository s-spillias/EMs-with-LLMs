A critical missing component in oceanic mixed layer models is light limitation. Phytoplankton growth in the mixed layer is co-limited by both nutrients AND light availability. The current model only considers nutrient

Add light limitation to phytoplankton growth using a multiplicative limitation framework. This represents the ecological reality that phytoplankton need both nutrients and light for photosynthesis. I'll implement this

This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                                                                                                     

 1 Light decreases exponentially with depth due to water absorption and phytoplankton self-shading                                                                                                                          
 2 Phytoplankton growth is limited by the minimum of light and nutrient availability                                                                                                                                        
 3 This creates realistic bloom dynamics and depth-dependent productivity
