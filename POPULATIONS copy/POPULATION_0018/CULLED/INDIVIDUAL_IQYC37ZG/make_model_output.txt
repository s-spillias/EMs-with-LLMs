Current Model Structure:                                                                                                                                                                                                    

The model implements a classic NPZ (Nutrient-Phytoplankton-Zooplankton) model with:                                                                                                                                         

 • Michaelis-Menten nutrient uptake by phytoplankton                                                                                                                                                                        
 • Holling Type II functional response for zooplankton grazing                                                                                                                                                              
 • Linear and quadratic zooplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                                               
 • Nutrient recycling from both phytoplankton and zooplankton mortality                                                                                                                                                     

Key Observation:                                                                                                                                                                                                            

Without residuals data, I'm analyzing the model structure itself. The current model assumes constant maximum growth and grazing rates (r_max and g_max), which is a simplification. In real oceanic systems, phytoplankton  
growth is often co-limited by both nutrients AND light availability, especially in the mixed layer where light varies with depth and season.                                                                                

Missing Ecological Process:                                                                                                                                                                                                 

The model lacks light limitation of phytoplankton growth. In the oceanic mixed layer (the PROJECT CONTEXT), light is a critical limiting factor alongside nutrients. Phytoplankton growth should be limited by the minimum  
of nutrient and light availability (Liebig's Law of the Minimum), or more realistically, by a multiplicative interaction of both factors.                                                                                   

Proposed Improvement:                                                                                                                                                                                                       

Add light-limited phytoplankton growth using a multiplicative light limitation term. This is ecologically justified because:                                                                                                

 1 The mixed layer experiences variable light conditions                                                                                                                                                                    
 2 Light and nutrient co-limitation is well-documented in marine systems                                                                                                                                                    
 3 This can explain seasonal bloom dynamics and depth-dependent productivity                                                                                                                                                

I'll implement this using a simple light attenuation model where light decreases with phytoplankton self-shading, combined with a saturating light response function.
