 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +The Holling Type III response introduces a sigmoidal (S-shaped) relationship between prey density (phytoplankton) and consumption rate (grazing). The mathematical form will be changed from `g_max * P / (K_P + P)` (Typ 
 II) to `g_max * P^2 / (K_P^2 + P^2)` (Type III).                                                                                                                                                                           
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +This change is justified by the following ecological principles:                                                                                                                                                          
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +1.  **Prey Refuge:** At low phytoplankton concentrations, the quadratic term in the numerator (`P^2`) causes the grazing rate to be very low, creating an effective refuge for the phytoplankton population. This prevent 
 zooplankton from driving phytoplankton to extinction and promotes coexistence.                                                                                                                                             
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +2.  **Predator Behavior:** The sigmoidal shape models prey switching. It implies that when the primary food source (phytoplankton) is rare, the predator (zooplankton) either switches to other food sources (not         
 explicitly modeled, but the effect is captured) or reduces its search effort, leading to a disproportionately low predation rate.                                                                                          
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +3.  **System Stability:** By providing a low-density refuge for the prey, the Holling Type III response often leads to more stable predator-prey oscillations and prevents the boom-bust cycles from driving populations  
 zero, which is a common issue in simpler NPZ models.                                                                                                                                                                       
 +                                                                                                                                                                                                                          
 +This modification represents a "higher-order mathematical representation" that is well-established in ecological theory and provides a more nuanced and realistic depiction of the grazing interaction, which is a critic 
 driver of plankton population dynamics. The parameter `K_P` is retained but now represents the phytoplankton concentration at which the grazing rate reaches half its maximum within this new sigmoidal functional form.
