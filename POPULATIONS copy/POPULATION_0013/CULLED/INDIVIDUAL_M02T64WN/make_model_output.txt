The current model provides a solid foundation. It correctly implements selective predation on fast- and slow-growing corals using a multi-species Holling Type II functional response. The drivers for population growth

However, the mechanism for the population "bust" is oversimplified. The current density-dependent mortality term (m_dd_cots) is a generic proxy for all processes that limit populations at high density, such as disease or
resource competition. It does not explicitly link COTS survival to the availability of their food source (coral). In reality, starvation following the depletion of coral is a primary driver of COTS outbreak collapse.    

2. Proposed Ecological Improvement                                                                                                                                                                                          

To better capture the full boom-bust cycle, I will introduce an explicit starvation-induced mortality mechanism. This creates a direct feedback loop where COTS mortality increases as total coral cover decreases. This    
change is critical for realistically modeling the decline phase of an outbreak.                                                                                                                                             

The COTS mortality calculation will be updated to include a term where mortality is highest at zero coral cover and decreases exponentially as coral becomes more abundant. This requires two new parameters:               

 â€¢ m_starve: The maximum additional mortality rate due to starvation when coral is absent.
