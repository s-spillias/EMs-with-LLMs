 • Nutrient outbreak multiplier

However, reviewing the parameter values and ecological literature, I notice a critical missing mechanism: predator saturation during outbreaks.                                                                             

                                                                                                   3. Key Missing Process                                                                                                   

The current model has density-dependent mortality (dd_mortality) for adults, but this represents intraspecific competition/crowding stress, NOT predation release. During COTS outbreaks, a well-documented phenomenon is   
that predators (particularly the giant triton Charonia tritonis and humphead wrasse) become saturated - they cannot consume COTS fast enough when densities are high. This creates a critical threshold effect where:       

 • At LOW densities: predation controls COTS population                                                                                                                                                                     
 • At HIGH densities: predators are overwhelmed, COTS escape predation control → outbreak                                                                                                                                   

This predator saturation is a key mechanism explaining why outbreaks are so explosive once initiated and why they persist at high densities.                                                                                

                                                                                                    4. Parameter Review                                                                                                     

Looking at log_dd_mortality = -1.609 (0.2 m²/ind/year), this represents crowding mortality that INCREASES with density. But ecologically, we need the OPPOSITE for predation: mortality rate should DECREASE at high        
densities due to predator saturation.                                                                                                                                                                                       


                                                                                     Proposed Improvement: Predator Saturation Function                                                                                     

I'll add a Type III predator saturation response for juvenile mortality, replacing the simple constant mortality with a density-dependent predation term that saturates at high COTS densities. This creates the critical   
threshold behavior needed for outbreaks.                                                                                                                                                                                    

Mathematical form:                                                                                                                                                                                                          

                                                                                                                                                                                                                            
 predation_mortality = max_predation_rate * (juvenile_density^2) / (predation_half_sat^2 + juvenile_density^2)                                                                                                              
                                                                                                                                                                                                                            

This creates:                                                                                                                                                                                                               

 • Strong predation control at low densities (quadratic increase)                                                                                                                                                           
 • Saturation at high densities (predators overwhelmed)                                                                                                                                                                     
 • A critical escape threshold where COTS can "break through" predation control
