    • The nutrient outbreak mechanism exists but doesn't interact with coral-mediated refuge effects                                                                                                                        

Proposed Improvement: Coral-Mediated Juvenile Refuge Effect                                                                                                                                                                 

This addresses the PROJECT CONTEXT by adding a critical positive feedback mechanism that can trigger and sustain outbreaks. When coral cover is high, juvenile COTS have more hiding places from predators (fish, other     
invertebrates), leading to higher survival rates. This creates a boom cycle: more coral → more juvenile survival → more adults → more reproduction → outbreak. The bust follows when coral is depleted.                     

This is ecologically well-documented and mathematically represents a state-dependent mortality rate for juveniles.
